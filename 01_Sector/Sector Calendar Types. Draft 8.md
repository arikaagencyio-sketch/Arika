**1\. Core Types of Sector Calendars**

**1\. Demand Calendars (Customer Behavior)**

These define **when people are most likely to buy, engage, or act**.

  * E-commerce → holiday spikes (Black Friday, Christmas) 
  * Fitness → January (resolutions), pre-summer 
  * Education → back-to-school seasons 
  * Agriculture → planting/harvest cycles 



👉 This is where **revenue timing** comes from.

**2\. Event Calendars (Attention Peaks)**

These are **external moments that concentrate attention**.

  * Industry expos, conferences 
  * Cultural moments (festivals, holidays) 
  * Product launch cycles 
  * Sports seasons 



👉 These drive **marketing timing + visibility leverage**

**3\. Operational Calendars (Internal Execution)**

These are **how businesses actually run behind the scenes**.

  * Product development cycles 
  * Inventory restocking cycles 
  * Hiring cycles 
  * Budgeting & planning cycles (quarterly, yearly) 



👉 This determines **capacity + delivery ability**

**4\. Financial Calendars (Money Flow)**

These define **when money moves and decisions happen**.

  * Budget approvals 
  * Fiscal year starts/ends 
  * Investor reporting periods 
  * Procurement cycles 



👉 This controls **when deals actually close**

**5\. Content & Media Calendars**

These are **attention distribution systems**.

  * Social media posting cycles 
  * Campaign rollouts 
  * Content themes tied to seasons/events 



👉 This drives **consistent visibility and narrative control**

**6\. Regulatory Calendars**

Often ignored—but critical in some sectors.

  * Compliance deadlines 
  * Tax filing periods 
  * Policy changes 
  * Licensing renewals 



👉 This can **block or unlock entire operations**

**7\. Innovation / Trend Cycles**

These are **when the sector evolves**.

  * Tech releases 
  * Trend waves (AI, sustainability, etc.) 
  * Consumer behavior shifts 



👉 This determines **future positioning**
