How should one map out a sector that carries a 360 mapping 

If you try to “map a sector” in a vague way, you’ll end up with a pretty diagram that nobody can actually use. A true 360° sector map is not visual first — it’s **structural, layered, and operational**. The goal is to make the sector _navigable, predictable, and monetizable_.

Here’s how to build it properly:

**1\. Start with the Sector Core (Why the sector exists)**

Define the _center of gravity_.

  * Core problem the sector solves 
  * Primary value exchange (money, attention, trust, data, etc.) 
  * Demand drivers (what keeps it alive) 



👉 Example:  
In fintech → trust + transaction efficiency

**2\. Map the Primary Layers (The 360 Spine)**

Think of the sector like a system with interacting layers:

**A. Demand Layer (Market Side)**

  * Customer segments (who buys) 
  * Psychographics (why they buy) 
  * Jobs-to-be-done 
  * Pain intensity levels 



**B. Supply Layer (Providers)**

  * Core service/product providers 
  * Subcategories (tiers, niches) 
  * Capability levels (premium vs commodity) 



**C. Value Chain Layer**

Break down _how value is created and delivered_ :

  * Upstream (raw inputs, infrastructure) 
  * Midstream (processing, transformation) 
  * Downstream (distribution, delivery) 



**D. Distribution Layer**

  * Channels (digital, physical, partnerships) 
  * Acquisition systems 
  * Attention flows 



**E. Monetization Layer**

  * Pricing models (subscription, transactional, licensing) 
  * Revenue flows 
  * Margins and cost structures 



**F. Influence Layer**

  * Media 
  * Brands 
  * Gatekeepers 
  * Cultural drivers 



**G. Infrastructure Layer**

  * Technology 
  * Legal/regulatory 
  * Financial rails 
  * Logistics 



**3\. Map the Actors (Who plays in the sector)**

For each layer, identify:

  * Clients (buyers) 
  * Operators (service providers) 
  * Partners (enablers) 
  * Competitors (direct/indirect) 
  * Gatekeepers (control access) 



👉 This is where your **client vs partner system plugs in**.

**4\. Map the Flows (What moves through the sector)**

A sector is not static — it’s flows:

  * Money flow 
  * Attention flow 
  * Data flow 
  * Trust flow 
  * Product/service flow 



👉 Draw how these move between actors and layers.

**5\. Map the Power Structure**

Every sector has hidden control points:

  * Who owns distribution? 
  * Who owns demand? 
  * Who owns trust? 
  * Who captures the most margin? 



👉 This is where strategy comes from.

**6\. Map the Lifecycle & Timing**

  * Entry points (how new players enter) 
  * Growth phases 
  * Saturation points 
  * Decline/disruption signals 



**7\. Map the Opportunities (Where you can win)**

Now convert the map into action:

  * Underserved segments 
  * Broken value chains 
  * Inefficient distribution 
  * Weak branding 
  * Pricing gaps 



**8\. Turn it into a System (Not a diagram)**

A real 360 sector map becomes:

  * A **decision system** → where to play 
  * A **product system** → what to offer 
  * A **sales system** → how to capture demand 
  * A **partner system** → who to align with 



**What Most People Miss (Critical)**

Most mappings fail because they ignore:

  * **Psychographics** (why behavior happens) 
  * **Power asymmetry** (who really controls things) 
  * **Flow dynamics** (movement vs static boxes) 
  * **Monetization logic** (how money actually moves)
