## The 6-Category Signal Framework

Six categories of signals indicate a prospect needs Marketing, Sales, Partner/Client Acquisition, or Automation services. Most operators only look for the first two; the framework's value is in pushing to all six, especially the 6th (predictive).

1. **Internal Signals** — pain inside the company (financial, operational, team). E.g., founder is the only person closing deals; team spends >40% of time on manual data entry.
2. **Market Signals** — external economic, industry, and technology shifts. E.g., cookie deprecation, AI adoption explosion, labor shortages.
3. **Behavioral Signals** — observable actions a prospect takes. E.g., visits pricing page repeatedly, downloads a relevant lead magnet, attends a relevant webinar.
4. **Stated Signals** — explicit things they say or write. E.g., "our close rate is too low," "we need to automate."
5. **Competitive Signals** — what competitors/peers are doing. E.g., a rival just hired a VP Sales or announced a major partnership.
6. **Predictive Signals** (leading indicators / pre-pain signals) — the category most operators miss. Appear *before* the prospect knows they have a problem: a funding round just closed, a new CMO/VP Sales was just hired, a job posting for "RevOps Manager" has been open 45+ days. Approaching on a predictive signal means zero competition — you look prescient, not like a vendor.

Two meta-properties to track across all six: **signal velocity** (how fast signals are appearing — one signal is weak, five in a week is urgent) and **signal density** (how many different categories are lit up simultaneously — multi-category hits are the highest-priority targets).

## The 90-Point Prospect Signal Scorecard

Platform-agnostic (works in a CRM, spreadsheet, or as mental notes on a call). Score each of the 6 categories 0-15 (5 signals per category, each scored 0-3: 0=none, 1=weak/indirect, 2=strong/direct, 3=clear/undeniable/urgent).

| Category | Max | Representative signals (5 per category, score 0-3 each) |
|---|---|---|
| Internal | 15 | High traffic/low conversion (<1%); founder is the only closer; team spends >40% time on manual admin; rising CAC or flat pipeline despite lead flow; no CRM used or CRM unused |
| Market | 15 | Cookie deprecation/privacy regulation hitting their industry; B2B buyers in their space increasingly self-educating; ecosystem marketplaces growing in their vertical; AI adoption surging among competitors; labor shortages/layoffs in their sector |
| Behavioral | 15 | Visited pricing/service page multiple times; downloaded a relevant lead magnet; attended a relevant webinar/event; watched a demo/case study; repeatedly engaged with content |
| Stated | 15 | "Our content isn't working" / "we need more leads"; "our close rate is too low" / "sales cycle is too long"; "we need partners/distribution"; "we're drowning in manual tasks"; publicly posted a pain point (LinkedIn, Twitter, community) |
| Competitive | 15 | Competitor launched a major content/partnership initiative; competitor hired a senior sales/marketing/partnership leader; competitor announced a major partnership; competitor automated a key process; industry consolidating (M&A activity) |
| Predictive | 15 | Just raised funding (within 6 months); just hired a new CMO/VP Sales/Head of Ops; just launched a new product or market; hiring rapidly in sales/ops/support; announced layoffs/budget cuts/restructuring |

**Grand total: /90.** Score bands: 0-22 Low (nurture, 90-day check-in) · 23-45 Medium (personalized value, book a call) · 46-67 High (direct outreach with a specific diagnosis and proposal) · 68-90 Critical (drop everything, full personalized pitch, offer a free audit/strategy session).

**Execution notes:** one signal in one category is interesting; the same strength of signal across three categories is urgent. Track velocity (re-score Medium-tier prospects every 30 days — signals decay). Match the offer to whichever category scores highest — don't pitch a marketing service to a prospect whose strongest signals are operational/automation.

## How this maps to the agency's 4 service lines

| Service line | Core signal pattern |
|---|---|
| Marketing | Traffic/awareness exists but isn't converting; market signal = old channels breaking (cookie deprecation, AI content saturation, rising CPMs) |
| Sales | Leads exist but pipeline stalls or founder is the sole closer; market signal = buyers are 70%+ self-educated before talking to sales, cold outreach underperforming |
| Partner / Client Acquisition | CAC rising faster than LTV, no formal referral program despite organic word-of-mouth; market signal = ecosystem/marketplace-led growth rising, investors pushing channel strategy |
| Automation | Team drowning in manual repetitive work, tools purchased but unintegrated/unused; market signal = AI adoption exploding, SaaS tool sprawl (130+ apps/company average), labor shortages |
