# CLAIMS SUBSTANTIATION POLICY

**Applies to:** everything Arika says publicly or to a prospect — website, social, decks,
proposals, audit reports, case studies, DMs, sales calls.

**The rule, in one line:**

> ## Arika does not make a claim it cannot evidence, on the day it makes it.

---

## 1. Why this policy exists, stated plainly

**Arika has zero clients and zero delivered engagements.** Every department OS records it:
`14_Audits_Diagnostics` §2, `15_Consulting_Advisory` §2, `16_Automation` §2,
`17_AI_Enablement` §2, `06_ClientPartner_Acquisition`.

That is the whole problem this policy exists to manage. **An agency with no track record has
nothing to substantiate a performance claim with**, and the temptation to imply one — through a
projection, a benchmark, a "typical client", a rounded-up range — is strongest exactly when
there is nothing real to point at.

**An unevidenced performance claim is not a marketing choice. It is advertising-law exposure**,
and `05_Sales`'s own `sales-risk-trust-governance` and `04_Content`'s `content-publishing-gate`
already route such claims here.

## 2. The four claim classes

| Class | Definition | Evidence required |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Factual, verifiable** | *"Arika is based in Kenya."* *"We use OpenArt and KIE.ai."* | The fact itself. Must be true **today** |
| **B — Capability** | *"We build CRM automation."* *"We run 7-sub-audit diagnostics."* | Arika must **actually be able to do it now**, not intend to |
| **C — Performance / outcome** | *"We increased revenue 40%."* *"Clients typically see X."* | **Real, attributable, documented results from real engagements.** 🔴 **Arika currently has NONE. Class C is banned outright until a real engagement produces one** |
| **D — Projection / estimate** | *"This audit identified $180K–$340K of annual leakage."* | **Stated method + stated assumptions + range + confidence label.** Never presented as achieved |

## 3. 🔴 Class C is banned. Not restricted — banned.

Until Arika has completed a real engagement with a real client and holds documented, attributable
results:

- **No case studies.** There are no cases.
- **No "our clients see…"**, no "typical results", no "up to X%".
- **No testimonials.** `07_Client_Success`'s `client-success-advocacy` agent exists to capture
  them — **it has nothing to capture yet, and must say so.**
- **No client logos.** There are no clients.
- **No implied track record** — including *"proven"*, *"battle-tested"*, *"trusted by"*, and
  *"we've helped hundreds"*.
- **No borrowed credibility.** A result from a previous employer, a case study from a tool
  vendor, or an industry benchmark presented as Arika's own is a Class C claim wearing a
  disguise.

**`20_Experience_Engineering/ARIKA_WEBSITE_PROJECT.md` records a Testimonials section left as an
intentional honest empty state.** That decision is this policy in practice, and it was made
before this policy existed. **Keep it.**

### What Arika may honestly say instead

- Class B capability claims — what it can do, framed as capability
- The **method**, in detail — the 7 sub-audits, the OEOS 12 phases, the immutables
- **Real, non-client proof**: the shipped website, the real generated imagery, the agent system,
  the operating documentation
- **The truth**: *"We are a new agency. Here is exactly how we work, and here is what we will
  guarantee."* Draft 28's own Entry-Tier risk reversal — **a clear revenue-optimization roadmap
  guaranteed regardless of what the audit finds** — is a **real, substantiable promise about
  process**. It is worth more than a borrowed statistic, and it is defensible.

## 4. Class D — projections, the class Arika actually lives in

Arika's offers are built on quantified findings. `14_Audits_Diagnostics`'s
`audits-quantification` **requires every finding to carry a quantified revenue impact** — that is
an immutable of offer #10, not an option.

**So Class D is unavoidable. It must therefore be disciplined:**

Every projection must carry, **in the same breath as the number**:

1. **The method** — the arithmetic, reproducible by the reader
2. **The inputs** — *the client's own figures*, named
3. **The assumptions** — every one, listed
4. **A range, never a point** — a single figure implies a precision that does not exist
5. **The period** — one-time / monthly / annual. *Annualising a one-time gain is the oldest way
   to inflate a number*
6. **A confidence label** — `measured` / `estimated` / `directional`, and **never exceeding the
   evidence** the data access allowed

**Never:**
- Present a projection as an achieved result
- Move a finding out of "unquantifiable" to make a total look better
  (`audits-quantification`'s explicit rule)
- Report `measured` because the client would prefer a hard number
- Use Arika's own benchmarks as a client's baseline — **there aren't any**

> **`14_Audits_Diagnostics`'s `total_identified_impact` is a string, not a number, on purpose:**
> *"$180K–$340K annually, estimated"*. **A bare total gets screenshotted, quoted back, and
> defended in a review call.** Make it carry its own caveats.

## 5. The disclaimer must match the sales conversation

**MSA Clause 8.3** disclaims any warranted outcome. **That clause is worth very little if a sales
call promised one.**

A contractual disclaimer contradicted by a pre-contract representation is exactly the fact
pattern that produces a misrepresentation claim — and **MSA Clause 12.6 expressly preserves
liability for fraudulent misrepresentation**, which no entire-agreement clause excludes.

> **The disclaimer protects the honest conversation. It does not repair a dishonest one.**

## 7. Approval

|---|---|

| B — Capability | Department owner |
| C — Performance | **🔴 Banned. If ever unbanned: owner + evidence file + counsel review** |
| D — Projection | Owner sign-off; **Class 3 where client-facing** (`audits-report-producer`) |

## 8. The evidence file

When Class C eventually becomes possible, **each claim needs a file before it is made**:
the client and engagement; the metric and its definition; the **baseline** and how it was
measured; the result and how it was measured; the **attribution basis** (why Arika's work caused
it, not correlation); the client's **written consent** to publish; and the date.

**No file, no claim.** *"The client said they were happy"* is not an evidence file.

## 9. Review

Reviewed `[annually]`, and **immediately upon the first real engagement completing** — the moment
Class C becomes live is the moment this policy's ban must be consciously lifted, with evidence,
rather than quietly forgotten.
