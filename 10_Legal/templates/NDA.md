# NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENTS

This file contains **two** NDAs. Use the right one:

| Use | Document |
|---|---|
| Both sides will share confidential information — e.g. a prospect discussing their business while Arika shares method | **[Part 1 — Mutual NDA](#part-1--mutual-nda)** |
| Only Arika is disclosing — e.g. sharing campaign concepts, creative ideas, or a proposal with someone who is not yet a client | **[Part 2 — One-Way NDA](#part-2--one-way-nda-arika-disclosing)** |

> **When an NDA is not the right tool.** If a client engagement is starting, **MSA Clause 6**
> covers confidentiality and an NDA is redundant — sign the MSA. NDAs are for the stage *before*
> an MSA exists, or for parties who will never sign one (a partner, a contractor, a prospect who
> doesn't convert).
>
> **Order of precedence:** if both an NDA and the MSA are in force, **the more protective term
> applies** (MSA Clause 6.7).

# PART 1 — MUTUAL NDA

**This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement** is made on `[DATE]` between:

**(1) `[ARIKA LEGAL ENTITY]`**, of `[REGISTERED ADDRESS]`, Kenya; and
**(2) `[OTHER PARTY LEGAL NAME]`**, of `[ADDRESS]`,

each a "**Party**". Each Party may act as **Discloser** or **Recipient**.

## 1. Purpose

1.1 The Parties wish to discuss **`[PURPOSE — e.g. a possible engagement for revenue
infrastructure work]`** (the "**Purpose**") and may disclose confidential information to each
other.

1.2 **Confidential Information may be used only for the Purpose.** Not for any other business,
not for evaluation of a competing option, not as a basis for doing the work in-house.

## 2. Confidential Information

2.1 "**Confidential Information**" means non-public information disclosed by a Discloser, in any
form, which is marked confidential or which a reasonable person would understand to be
confidential from its nature or the circumstances.

2.2 It includes, without limitation: business plans, financials, pricing, customer and pipeline
data, personal data, technology, source code, methods, strategies, **campaign and creative
concepts**, and the **existence and content of the Parties' discussions**.

2.3 **Marking is not required.** Information disclosed orally or visually is protected if it
would reasonably be understood as confidential. *A Discloser is encouraged, but not required, to
confirm it in writing within `[14]` days.*

> **Why 2.3 matters:** most real disclosure happens on a call, unmarked. A regime that protects
> only stamped documents protects almost nothing.

## 3. Obligations

3.1 The Recipient will: keep Confidential Information confidential; use it only for the Purpose;
protect it with at least the care it uses for its own confidential information, and no less than
reasonable care.

3.2 The Recipient may disclose it only to its personnel, professional advisers, and
**sub-processors/subcontractors** who need it for the Purpose and are bound by obligations at
least as protective. **The Recipient remains responsible for their compliance.**

3.3 The Recipient will not reverse-engineer, decompile, or attempt to derive the underlying
structure of anything disclosed.

3.4 The Recipient will not make copies beyond what the Purpose requires.

## 4. Exclusions

4.1 This Agreement does not apply to information that:
(a) is or becomes public other than through breach of this Agreement;
(b) the Recipient lawfully held before disclosure, free of any confidentiality duty;
(c) the Recipient independently develops **without use of or reference to** the Confidential
Information; or
(d) is lawfully received from a third party without restriction.

4.2 **The Recipient bears the burden of proving an exclusion applies**, with contemporaneous
written evidence.

## 5. Compelled disclosure

5.1 The Recipient may disclose where required by law, court order, or a regulator, provided it
(so far as lawful): gives the Discloser **prompt written notice** so the Discloser may seek
protective relief; discloses **only what is legally required**; and uses reasonable efforts to
obtain confidential treatment.

## 6. No licence, no obligation

6.1 **All Confidential Information remains the Discloser's property.** No licence or right is
granted, expressly or by implication, other than to use it for the Purpose.

6.2 Nothing obliges either Party to disclose anything, to proceed with any transaction, or to
refrain from dealing with third parties (subject to Clause 1.2).

6.3 **No warranty.** Confidential Information is provided "as is". Neither Party warrants its
accuracy or completeness, except as may later be agreed in a definitive contract.

## 7. Return and destruction

7.1 On the Discloser's written request, or when the Purpose ends, the Recipient will return or
destroy Confidential Information and certify destruction in writing if asked.

7.2 The Recipient may retain: copies required by law or regulation; and copies in routine
automated backups **which are not readily retrievable**. Retained copies remain subject to this
Agreement **for as long as they are retained** — including beyond the term in Clause 8.

## 8. Term

8.1 This Agreement starts on the date above and runs for `[2]` years.

8.2 Confidentiality obligations survive for `[3]` years from the date of each disclosure, and
**indefinitely for trade secrets and personal data**, for as long as the information remains a
trade secret or personal data.

> ⚠️ **Two different clocks — this is deliberate.** The *agreement* has a term (8.1); each
> *disclosure* has its own protection period (8.2). A disclosure made on the last day of the
> term is protected for `[3]` years from that day, not for one more day.

## 9. Personal data

9.1 If Confidential Information includes **personal data**, the Recipient will Process it only
for the Purpose and in accordance with applicable data-protection law, including Kenya's **Data
Protection Act 2019** and, where applicable, the **UK/EU GDPR**.

9.2 **An NDA is not a DPA.** If the Purpose involves a Party Processing personal data on the
other's behalf, the Parties will enter a **Data Processing Agreement**
([`DPA.md`](DPA.md)) **before that Processing begins**.

> ⚠️ **This clause exists because the mistake is common and expensive.** An NDA makes disclosure
> *confidential*; it does **not** make Processing *lawful*. If a prospect gives Arika a CRM
> export "under NDA" to look at before signing, **that is Processing personal data with no DPA
> and no s.48 transfer basis the moment it lands in a foreign tool.** See
> [`../LEGAL_RESEARCH.md`](../LEGAL_RESEARCH.md) §5. **Do not accept client data on an NDA
> alone.**

## 10. Remedies

10.1 The Parties agree that damages may not be an adequate remedy for breach, and that the
Discloser may seek **injunctive relief** in addition to any other remedy, without needing to
prove actual damage or post security.

## 11. General

11.1 **No partnership or agency** is created.
11.2 **Assignment** requires the other Party's written consent, except to a successor of
substantially all its business.
11.3 **Entire agreement** on its subject matter; supersedes prior understandings on
confidentiality.
11.4 **Variation** only in writing, signed by both.
11.5 **Severability** — an unenforceable provision is modified to the minimum extent necessary or
severed.
11.6 **Counterparts**, including electronic signature.
11.7 **Governing law:** `[GOVERNING LAW]`. **Jurisdiction:** `[exclusive/non-exclusive]` courts
of `[JURISDICTION]`.

# PART 2 — ONE-WAY NDA (ARIKA DISCLOSING)

**Use when Arika is sharing and the other side is not.** Typical: sharing campaign concepts,
creative ideas, an audit methodology, a proposal, or agent/system design with a prospect,
partner, contractor, or freelancer.

**This Non-Disclosure Agreement** is made on `[DATE]` between:

**(1) `[ARIKA LEGAL ENTITY]`**, of `[REGISTERED ADDRESS]`, Kenya ("**Arika**", the
**Discloser**); and
**(2) `[RECIPIENT LEGAL NAME]`**, of `[ADDRESS]` (the "**Recipient**").

## 1. Purpose

1.1 Arika will disclose confidential information to the Recipient for the purpose of **`[PURPOSE
— e.g. evaluating a proposed campaign concept]`** (the "**Purpose**").

1.2 **The Recipient may use it only for the Purpose.**

## 2. Confidential Information

2.1 "**Confidential Information**" means non-public information disclosed by Arika, in any form,
including: **campaign concepts, creative ideas, storyboards, narrative and messaging
architecture, positioning, audit methodology and findings, pricing, agent and system design,
operating documentation, and client information.**

2.2 Marking is not required (as Part 1, Clause 2.3).

## 3. 🔴 Ideas, concepts, and campaigns — the clause this document exists for

3.1 **Disclosure is not permission.** Arika's concepts, ideas, and campaign proposals are
disclosed **solely** so the Recipient may evaluate them for the Purpose. **The Recipient
acquires no right to use, adapt, execute, or commission any of them.**

3.2 **The Recipient will not** execute, or have a third party execute, any concept or campaign
disclosed by Arika, in whole or in **materially similar** form, **without a signed agreement
with Arika**.

3.3 **Independent development.** Part 1 Clause 4.1(c) applies — but the Recipient bears the
burden of proving, with contemporaneous written evidence, that it developed the material
independently **without reference to** Arika's disclosure.

3.4 Nothing prevents the Recipient from continuing to develop ideas it demonstrably held before
disclosure, or from working with others on the same **general subject matter** — provided it does
not use Arika's Confidential Information.

> ### Why this clause is drafted this way, and its honest limit
> The predictable harm to a creative agency is a prospect taking the pitch to a cheaper
> executor. **Clause 3.2 addresses it directly** by prohibiting execution rather than merely
> prohibiting disclosure — because a concept can be *executed* without ever being *disclosed* to
> anyone else.
>
> **But be realistic.** *"Materially similar"* is where these disputes live and die, and proving
> it is hard and expensive. **Clause 3.4 is deliberately included** so this is defensible rather
> than an overreaching restraint a court may decline to enforce — an unenforceable clause
> protects nothing. **The strongest protection is commercial, not legal: don't disclose your
> best concept before there's a signed SOW.** See
> [`IP_COPYRIGHT_TRADEMARK_TERMS.md`](IP_COPYRIGHT_TRADEMARK_TERMS.md) §2 — **ideas as such are
> generally not protected by copyright; their expression is.** This clause is contractual, and
> contract is the only real protection an idea has.

## 4. Obligations

4.1 As Part 1, Clause 3 (applied to the Recipient only).

4.2 The Recipient will not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without Arika's
prior written consent, except to personnel and advisers who need it for the Purpose and are bound
by equivalent obligations, for whom the Recipient remains responsible.

## 5. Exclusions, compelled disclosure, return, term

5.1 Part 1 Clauses **4, 5, 7 and 8** apply, reading "Recipient" for the Recipient and "Discloser"
for Arika.

## 6. No licence

6.1 **All Confidential Information, and all intellectual property in it, remains Arika's.** No
licence is granted. Evaluation is not use.

## 7. Remedies and general

7.1 Part 1 Clauses **10 and 11** apply.

## Notes (delete before sending)

- **Pick one part.** Sending a file containing both looks unconsidered.
- **A mutual NDA is usually the easier ask** — a one-way NDA in Arika's favour signals distrust
  to a prospect and is more often refused. Part 2's real home is contractors, freelancers, and
  partners.
- **Clause 3.2 (Part 2) is the commercially important clause.** Expect it to be negotiated.
- **Never accept client personal data under an NDA alone** — Clause 9.2 and
  `LEGAL_RESEARCH.md` §5.

  indefinitely regardless.
