What are the legal structure of a client and that of a business

You’re mixing two things that don’t naturally mirror each other: a **business has a legal structure** , but a **client doesn’t have a “client legal structure”** in the same formal sense. A client only has a _legal identity and capacity_. So if you try to force symmetry here, you’ll create confusion in your system.

Let’s cleanly separate them and then reconnect them in a way that actually helps your client system.

**1\. Legal Structure of a Business**

This is **how the business is legally formed and recognized**. It determines liability, taxation, ownership, and compliance.

**Core business structures:**

  * **Sole Proprietorship**
    * One owner 
    * No legal separation between owner and business 
    * High personal liability 
  * **Partnership**
    * Two or more owners 
    * Shared responsibility and liability (unless limited) 
  * **Limited Liability Company (LLC) / Private Limited Company**
    * Separate legal entity 
    * Owners protected from personal liability 
    * Flexible structure (very common) 
  * **Corporation**
    * Fully separate legal entity 
    * Shareholders, directors, officers 
    * More complex but scalable 
  * **Nonprofit / NGO**
    * Purpose-driven, not profit-distribution focused 
    * Governed by specific regulations 



**Why this matters for your client system:**

Your structure defines:

  * What contracts you can issue 
  * How you price and invoice 
  * Your risk exposure 
  * Your compliance obligations 



**2\. “Legal Structure” of a Client (Correct Framing)**

A client doesn’t have a “client structure” — they have a **legal identity type**.

**Client legal identities:**

  * **Individual (Natural Person)**
    * One person hiring you 
    * Contracts signed personally 
  * **Sole Trader**
    * Individual operating a business (not separate legally) 
  * **Company / Corporation**
    * Separate legal entity 
    * Requires authorized signatory 
  * **Partnership**
    * Multiple individuals legally tied 
  * **Government / Institution**
    * Highly regulated procurement processes

**2\. The Map (Applied Across All Client Types)**

**1\. Legal Person (Who exactly are we dealing with?)**

Defines the legal existence.

  * Individual (natural person) 
  * Registered business (company, LLC, etc.) 
  * Partnership 
  * Institution / Government 



**Mutual clarity outcome:**  
→ “Who is legally responsible in this relationship?”

**2\. Authority (Who can bind this client legally?)**

This is where most businesses get burned.

  * Owner (sole trader) 
  * Director / Officer (company) 
  * Authorized employee 
  * Procurement officer (institution) 



**Key question:**  
→ “Can this person legally commit the client?”

**Mutual clarity outcome:**  
→ No “I need to check with my boss” after agreement

**3\. Obligation (Who pays, and under what terms?)**

Defines financial responsibility.

  * Who is invoiced? 
  * Payment terms (advance, net-30, milestone) 
  * Currency and method 
  * Penalties / late fees 



**Mutual clarity outcome:**  
→ No confusion on money flow

**4\. Risk Profile (What happens if things go wrong?)**

This is the boundary layer.

  * Liability limits 
  * Refund conditions 
  * Scope boundaries 
  * Dependencies (what client must provide) 



**Mutual clarity outcome:**  
→ No emotional disputes—only pre-agreed rules

**5\. Jurisdiction (Which laws govern this?)**

Critical, especially if you scale or go cross-border.

  * Country / region of client 
  * Governing law in contract 
  * Dispute resolution method 



**Mutual clarity outcome:**  
→ Where and how conflicts are handled
