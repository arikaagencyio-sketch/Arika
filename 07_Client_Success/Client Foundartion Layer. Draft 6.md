You’re close—but “onboarding” is actually _after_ the client has already said yes. So it’s not _before_ sales/marketing—it’s part of delivery.

What you’re really trying to map is: **what exists _before_ marketing and sales even begin**—the _client foundation layer_.

Here’s the clean way to structure it 👇

**🧠 CLIENT LAYER (Before Marketing & Sales)**

This is where most people get it wrong.  
Before you attract or sell to a client, you must define and engineer them.

**1\. Client Definition (WHO)**

This is the raw identification layer.

  * Target client segments 
  * Industry / niche 
  * Company size / stage 
  * Decision-maker profiles 



👉 Example:

  * Startup founders vs enterprise managers 
  * B2B vs B2C 
  * High-ticket vs volume buyers 



**2\. Client Intelligence (UNDERSTANDING)**

This is deeper than demographics—it’s how they think.

  * Pain points (urgent vs passive) 
  * Goals and desired outcomes 
  * Buying triggers 
  * Objections and fears 
  * Current alternatives they use 



👉 This is where you build _psychographics_

**3\. Client Economics (VALUE CAPACITY)**

Not all clients are equal.

  * Budget / purchasing power 
  * Lifetime value (LTV) 
  * Cost to acquire (CAC potential) 
  * Profitability per segment 



👉 This determines:

  * Who is worth targeting 
  * Who gets premium vs low-tier offers 



**4\. Client Journey Mapping (BEHAVIOR)**

Before marketing, you need to understand:

  * Where they hang out (channels) 
  * How they discover solutions 
  * How long they take to decide 
  * What steps they go through before buying 



👉 This becomes your:

  * Funnel logic 
  * Channel strategy 



**5\. Client Fit & Qualification (FILTERING)**

Not every client should enter your system.

  * Ideal vs non-ideal clients 
  * Red flags 
  * Qualification criteria 



👉 This prevents:

  * Bad clients 
  * Churn 
  * Operational chaos later 



**6\. Client Experience Blueprint (PRE-DESIGN)**

This is where onboarding _starts to be designed_ —but not executed yet.

  * What should the experience feel like? 
  * Speed, communication style, expectations 
  * Level of support 



👉 This ensures:

  * Consistency after the sale 



**🔁 Where Onboarding Actually Fits**

Here’s the correct order:

  1. Client Definition 
  2. Client Intelligence 
  3. Client Economics 
  4. Client Journey Mapping 
  5. Marketing 
  6. Sales 
  7. ✅ **Onboarding (first post-sale step)**
  8. Delivery / Fulfillment 
  9. Retention / Expansion 



**⚠️ Key Insight**

If you skip the **pre-client layer** , then:

  * Your marketing attracts the wrong people 
  * Your sales struggles to convert 
  * Your onboarding becomes messy 
  * Your delivery breaks 



**🧩 Simple Mental Model**

Think of it like this:

  * **Before sales = Design the client**
  * **During sales = Acquire the client**
  * **After sales = Deliver to the client**
