In order to build the right cadence for sales who plays what role and why and what are the principle, roles and stratergies for each

You don’t build sales cadence by just assigning roles and hoping activity happens. That’s how you get noise instead of momentum. Cadence works when **roles, timing, and intent are tightly engineered together** —each touchpoint has a purpose, and each role advances the deal in a specific way.

Let’s break it down into **principles → roles → cadence strategy per role** so you can actually operationalize it.

**1\. Core Principles Behind Sales Cadence**

These guide _everything_ , regardless of team size:

**1) Relevance over frequency**

More touchpoints don’t mean better outcomes. Every interaction must answer:  
**“Why should the buyer care right now?”**

**2) Multi-threading (not single contact dependency)**

Deals don’t close through one person. Cadence must involve:

  * Economic buyer 
  * Technical buyer 
  * End user 



**3) Channel orchestration**

Not just email sequences:

  * Calls 
  * LinkedIn / social 
  * WhatsApp (regionally powerful in places like Kenya) 
  * Events / in-person 



**4) Timing based on buying signals**

Cadence shouldn’t be static. It should flex based on:

  * Engagement (opens, replies) 
  * Behavior (site visits, demos) 
  * Urgency signals 



**5) Value layering**

Each touch adds **new value** , not repetition:

  * Insight 
  * Proof 
  * Risk reduction 
  * Urgency 



**2\. Key Roles in Sales Cadence (Who Plays What & Why)**

**1) Sales Development Rep (SDR / BDR)**

**Role:** Create and qualify opportunities  
**Why:** They initiate momentum and feed the pipeline

**What they do in cadence:**

  * First contact (cold outreach) 
  * Follow-ups 
  * Qualification conversations 



**Cadence Strategy:**

  * High activity, short cycles (7–14 days) 
  * Mix of: 
    * Call → Email → Social → Call 
  * Focus on: 
    * Pain identification 
    * Curiosity creation 



👉 Goal: **Get the meeting, not close the deal**

**2) Account Executive (AE)**

**Role:** Convert opportunities into revenue  
**Why:** They drive the deal from interest → decision

**What they do in cadence:**

  * Discovery calls 
  * Product demos 
  * Objection handling 
  * Negotiation 



**Cadence Strategy:**

  * Medium frequency, high depth 
  * Structured stages: 
    * Discovery → Solution → Validation → Close 
  * Each touchpoint: 
    * Answers a buying question 



👉 Goal: **Advance the deal every interaction**

**3) Sales Manager / Team Lead**

**Role:** Enforce cadence discipline and optimize performance  
**Why:** Without control, cadence breaks down into inconsistency

**What they do:**

  * Pipeline reviews 
  * Deal coaching 
  * Performance tracking 



**Cadence Strategy:**

  * Weekly deal reviews 
  * Daily activity monitoring 
  * Identify: 
    * Stalled deals 
    * Weak messaging 
    * Poor timing 



👉 Goal: **Keep the system predictable and scalable**

**4) Customer Success Manager (CSM)**

**Role:** Retain and expand accounts  
**Why:** Sales doesn’t end at closing—this is where real revenue compounds

**What they do in cadence:**

  * Onboarding 
  * Check-ins 
  * Upsell / cross-sell 



**Cadence Strategy:**

  * Long-cycle, relationship-driven 
  * Scheduled touchpoints: 
    * 30-60-90 day reviews 
    * Quarterly business reviews 



👉 Goal: **Turn customers into repeat revenue + advocates**

**5) Marketing (Often Ignored in Cadence)**

**Role:** Support and warm up the buyer journey  
**Why:** Sales alone cannot create demand at scale

**What they do:**

  * Content 
  * Campaigns 
  * Lead nurturing 



**Cadence Strategy:**

  * Parallel to sales outreach: 
    * Retargeting ads 
    * Email nurturing 
    * Case studies 



👉 Goal: **Increase conversion probability before sales even speaks**

**3\. Cadence Strategy by Stage (How Roles Work Together)**

**Stage 1: Prospecting (SDR-led)**

  * Day 1: Call + Email 
  * Day 3: Follow-up email with insight 
  * Day 5: LinkedIn touch 
  * Day 7: Call 



**Principle:** Break through noise

**Stage 2: Qualification (SDR → AE handoff)**

  * SDR confirms: 
    * Need 
    * Budget 
    * Authority 
  * AE enters with: 
    * Deeper conversation 
    * Business problem framing 



**Principle:** Smooth transition, no repetition

**Stage 3: Deal Development (AE-led)**

  * Demo → Follow-up → Objection handling 
  * Share: 
    * Case studies 
    * ROI insights 
    * Implementation clarity 



**Principle:** Reduce uncertainty

**Stage 4: Closing**

  * AE + Manager involvement 
  * Cadence tightens: 
    * Frequent check-ins 
    * Decision nudges 



**Principle:** Create urgency without pressure

**Stage 5: Post-Sale (CSM-led)**

  * Onboarding cadence 
  * Value reinforcement 
  * Expansion conversations 



**Principle:** Deliver what was promised (or churn happens)
