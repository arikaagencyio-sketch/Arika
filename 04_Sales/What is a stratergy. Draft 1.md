What is a stratergy

A **strategy** is a clear plan of action designed to achieve a specific goal, especially over the long term. It’s about deciding **what you want to accomplish and how you’re going to get there** , while considering challenges, resources, and opportunities.

Think of it like this:

  * **Goal** = what you want (e.g., increase sales) 
  * **Strategy** = the overall approach you choose (e.g., target high-value clients and build relationships) 
  * **Tactics** = the specific actions you take (e.g., cold emails, demos, follow-ups) 



**Simple definition:**

A strategy is **the big-picture plan that guides your decisions and actions**.

**Example in sales:**

  * Goal: Increase revenue 
  * Strategy: Focus on upselling existing customers instead of only finding new ones 
  * Tactics: Offer bundle discounts, personalized offers, loyalty rewards 



A good strategy helps you:

  * Stay focused 
  * Use resources efficiently 
  * Make consistent decisions 
  * Adapt when things change

how to develop sales strateries

Developing a strong sales strategy isn’t about guessing—it’s about making deliberate choices based on your market, customers, and goals. Here’s a practical way to build one that actually works:

**1\. Start with a clear goal**

Be specific about what you want:

  * Increase revenue by 20% 
  * Close more high-value clients 
  * Enter a new market 



If the goal is vague, the strategy will be weak.

**2\. Understand your target customer**

You need to know:

  * Who they are (industry, size, income level) 
  * Their problems (pain points) 
  * What motivates them to buy 



Create a simple **ideal customer profile (ICP)** so your efforts are focused.

**3\. Analyze your product or service**

Ask yourself:

  * What problem does it solve? 
  * What makes it different from competitors? 
  * Why should someone choose you? 



This becomes your **value proposition**.

**4\. Study your competition**

Look at:

  * Their pricing 
  * Their sales approach 
  * Their strengths and weaknesses 



Then position yourself clearly (cheaper, better quality, faster, more personalized, etc.).

**5\. Choose your sales approach (your core strategy)**

Decide how you’ll win customers. For example:

  * Focus on **high-volume, low-cost sales**
  * Focus on **premium, relationship-based sales**
  * Focus on **repeat customers and upselling**



This is your main direction—don’t try to do everything.

**6\. Build your sales funnel**

Map the journey:

  1. Lead generation (finding prospects) 
  2. Qualification (are they a good fit?) 
  3. Pitch/demo 
  4. Closing 
  5. Follow-up/retention 



Each stage should have a clear plan.

**7\. Pick your channels**

Decide where your customers are and how you’ll reach them:

  * Social media 
  * Email outreach 
  * Referrals 
  * Cold calling 
  * Partnerships 



Focus on a few that work best instead of spreading yourself thin.

**8\. Set metrics (very important)**

Track things like:

  * Number of leads 
  * Conversion rate 
  * Average deal size 
  * Sales cycle time 



If you don’t measure, you can’t improve.

**9\. Train and align your team**

Make sure everyone:

  * Understands the strategy 
  * Uses the same messaging 
  * Follows the same process 



Consistency is key in sales.

**10\. Test and improve**

No strategy is perfect from the start.

  * Track results 
  * Identify what’s working 
  * Adjust quickly
