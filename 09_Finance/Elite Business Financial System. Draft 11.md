**1.6 CORE FINANCIAL CONCEPTS**

**Revenue**

Top-line money entering the business.

Revenue = Total Sales Generated

**Gross Profit**

Revenue minus direct production costs.

Measures production efficiency.

**Operating Profit**

Profit after operational expenses.

Operating Profit = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses

Measures operational performance.

**Net Profit**

Final profit after all costs.

Net Profit = Revenue - Total Expenses

Measures ultimate profitability.

**EBITDA**

Earnings before:

  * Interest
  * Taxes
  * Depreciation
  * Amortization



Used to measure operational cash-generation capacity.

**Burn Rate**

Monthly cash consumption.

**Runway**

How long cash reserves last.

**Liquidity**

Ability to meet short-term obligations.

**Solvency**

Long-term financial viability.

**Working Capital**

Measures operational financial flexibility.

**Financial Leverage**

Using debt to increase returns.

Good leverage:

  * Productive
  * Controlled
  * Cash-flow supported



Bad leverage:

  * Speculative
  * Unsecured by cash flow
  * Overextended



**Financial Resilience**

Ability to survive:

  * Recessions
  * Revenue shocks
  * Supply disruptions
  * Market volatility
