**1\. What “Sector Intelligence” Actually Is in Branding**

Think of each sector as having its own **operating system of trust and value**.

  * Healthcare → trust = credibility + safety 
  * Luxury → trust = status + exclusivity 
  * Tech → trust = innovation + usability 
  * Finance → trust = stability + authority 
  * Education → trust = transformation + credibility 



So sector intelligence =

**A structured understanding of how a market defines value, trust, risk, and differentiation**

**2\. The Sector Intelligence Framework (Core Architecture)**

To make this usable, you need a **repeatable intelligence model** you apply to any sector.

**Layer 1: Value Logic (What matters here?)**

  * What does the customer actually buy? 
  * Functional value vs emotional vs social value 



Example:

  * SaaS → efficiency, scalability 
  * Real estate → security, future value 
  * Fashion → identity, belonging 



**Layer 2: Trust Mechanics (How belief is built)**

Every sector has a **different proof system**.

  * Healthcare → certifications, expertise 
  * Finance → track record, numbers 
  * E-commerce → reviews, convenience 
  * B2B → case studies, ROI 



👉 This directly shapes:

  * messaging 
  * website structure 
  * sales flow 



**Layer 3: Risk Profile (What the customer fears)**

This is where most branding fails.

  * High-risk sectors (finance, health) → need reassurance-heavy branding 
  * Low-risk sectors (snacks, entertainment) → can be bold, playful 



Ask:

  * What can go wrong for the customer? 
  * What’s at stake emotionally, financially, socially? 



**Layer 4: Decision Dynamics (How people choose)**

Is the decision:

  * Fast or slow? 
  * Individual or committee-based? 
  * Rational or emotional? 



Examples:

  * B2B SaaS → long, multi-stakeholder 
  * Food → quick, emotional 
  * Luxury → slow, identity-driven 



**Layer 5: Category Codes (What signals “this belongs here”)**

Every sector has **visual and verbal norms**.

  * Law firms → dark colors, serif fonts 
  * Tech → clean UI, minimalism 
  * Beauty → aspirational visuals 
  * Finance → blue, structured layouts 



👉 You must decide:

  * Follow codes (to build trust) 
  * Break codes (to stand out) 



**Layer 6: Competitive Narrative Space**

What stories dominate the sector?

Example:

  * Tech → “innovation” 
  * Fitness → “transformation” 
  * Banking → “security” 



Your job:

  * Map the narratives 
  * Find the gap 



**Layer 7: Regulatory & Structural Constraints**

Some sectors are not free.

  * Finance, healthcare → compliance-heavy 
  * Alcohol, gambling → restricted messaging 
  * Education → accreditation signals 



👉 This shapes:

  * tone 
  * claims 
  * campaigns
