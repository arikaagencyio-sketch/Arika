## 1. Identity

ClientPartner Acquisition is **the acquisition discipline** — the engineering of how
revenue relationships and leverage relationships are found, qualified, earned, and
compounded.

The source names this system **CPAROS** — the *Client & Partner Acquisition Revenue
Operating System* (`ClientPartner Acquisition System. Draft 2.md`) — and, at its
runtime layer, **ClientPartnerOS** (`ClientPartner Os Architure. Draft 8.md`). Its
founding claim:

> **"Acquisition is not a process — it is a revenue-producing system architecture
> composed of interconnected subsystems."**

> The system exists to transform **random outreach → institutional acquisition
> infrastructure.**

This department does not "do outreach." It engineers acquisition systems.

## 2. Dual Scope — and the correction that created this file

**This department is dual-scope, weighted to clients** (owner-confirmed, 2026-07-15).

### 2.1 Client-facing — **primary**
Arika designs, installs, and operates **acquisition systems for its clients**: their
client-acquisition engine, their partner ecosystem, their CRM and pipeline, their
psychographic and trust architecture. This is a **service line**, and the source was
written to produce exactly that:

> **FINAL GOAL:** *"Produce a complete Client & Partner Acquisition Revenue
> Architecture Blueprint that can be used to: **Sell high-ticket acquisition
> systems**; **Structure agency service tiers**; **Monetize partnerships at scale**"*
> — `Draft 2`, the owner's own brief

> *"…the CRM is what you get maybe from **a client's audit**… as well as now the
> deliverables that **you are actually giving the client**… in client/partner
> acquisition **for self and any scope of sectors**"* — `Draft 13`, the owner's own words

**"For self and any scope of sectors"** is the dual scope, stated by the owner, in the
source. It is the load-bearing sentence of this department.

### 2.2 Agency-facing — **secondary, and partner-only**
The same discipline pointed at Arika itself. Here the scope is **the Partner pipeline
only**: sourcing, qualifying, enabling, and expanding Arika's own referral /
distribution / capability / credibility / strategic / capital / ecosystem partners.

**Arika's own *client* acquisition belongs to Sales (05) and Marketing (03)/Content
(04), not here.** This is the correct, surviving half of the 2026-06-30 reconciliation.

### 2.3 The correction (why §2.2 is not the whole department)
A reconciliation pass on 2026-06-30 resolved this department's mandate to *"the Partner
pipeline only"* and marked Drafts 5, 7, and 9 **"superseded"** as duplicates of
Marketing/Content. That pass reached the right conclusion **about the agency dimension**
and then applied it to the whole department, because it assumed every draft described
acquisition *for Arika*.

The drafts describe acquisition **for whoever the system is pointed at**:
- `Draft 3` opens with a **12-question intake** asking a business to identify its own
  model (*"Agency / Consultancy / SaaS / Media company / Holding company…"*) and its
  desired clients (*"NGOs / Governments / Creators / E-commerce / Local businesses"*).
  Arika knows what Arika is; Arika's ICP is confirmed 3-tier B2B SaaS (Sector, 01).
  **This is a client discovery instrument.**
- `Draft 5` specifies a deploy stack of **ClickFunnels / HubSpot / GoHighLevel /
  ActiveCampaign** and a human layer of *"content operator, media buyer, sales rep /
  closer, systems manager."* Arika runs ClickUp + Zoho + Notion and is **solo + AI**.
  **This is a client's stack and a client's team.**
- `Draft 7`'s worked examples are *"NGO leaders building donor pipelines"* and
  *"E-commerce brands stuck at fulfillment bottlenecks."*

**Drafts 5, 7, and 9 are hereby un-superseded** and reclassified as **client-facing
delivery doctrine**. They never competed with Marketing (03) — Marketing markets
**Arika**; this department builds acquisition systems **for clients**. Different
customer, no overlap.

### 2.4 The Client→Partner bridge (both scopes meet here)

Arika's clients may become Arika's partners. This is real and named

## 3. The Core Separation — the supreme law of this department

The single most-repeated rule across the corpus:

> ### **The system MUST NEVER confuse client logic with partner logic.**

| | **Client** | **Partner** |
|---|---|---|
| Engine | **Revenue Extraction Engine** — you sell to them | **Distribution / Leverage Engine** — you do **not** sell to them |
| Relationship | Revenue | Strategic leverage |
| Orientation | Transaction | Ecosystem |
| Function | Solves buyer problem | Expands operational capability |
| Shape | Usually linear | Usually networked |
| Trust cycle | Shorter | Longer |
| Exchange | Value | Capability |
| Monetization | Direct | Indirect **and** direct |
| Structure | Funnel-based | Ecosystem-based |
| Logic | Sales logic | Alliance logic |
| Focus | Conversion | Compounding |
| Psychology | Buyer psychology | Institutional psychology |
| Effort scaling | Linear | Exponential |
| Control | High | **Shared** |
| Risk | Low per deal | **High if misaligned** |

*(`Draft 8` "The Core Distinction"; `Draft 3` §1 and §4)*

Because **clients generate revenue** and **partners generate expansion capacity**, the
architecture requires **dual-runtime cognition** — two engines, never one merged
pipeline. `Draft 3`'s warning: *"If you don't separate them cleanly, you'll build one
messy pipeline that fails at both revenue and scale."*

**The named failure mode** (`Draft 4`):
> *"Pitch partners like clients → they disengage. Treat clients like partners → they
> don't convert."*

**The model:** *Clients = Fuel · Partners = Engines · Your system = Vehicle.*
*No fuel → you don't move. No engine → you don't scale.*

## 4. The Trust Doctrine — non-negotiable

Verbatim from `Draft 8` (`/constitution/trust_doctrine.md`), the department's own
highest-authority language:

> - **Trust MUST be earned before extraction.**
> - **The system MUST prioritize relationship equity over short-term conversion.**
> - **The system MUST NOT manipulate psychological vulnerabilities.**
> - **Every acquisition interaction MUST increase long-term trust position.**

**Partner funnels are trust funnels, not sales funnels** (`Draft 6`). A partner risks
**reputation, relationships, audience trust, and strategic positioning** by associating
with you. Therefore:

> *"High-value partners are not acquired through begging. They are attracted through
> leverage, positioning, proof, and opportunity."*

Credibility gates entry **before** any pitch. This is doctrine, not tactics.

**Note on the third MUST.** *"MUST NOT manipulate psychological vulnerabilities"* is a
hard constraint on this department specifically, because this is the department that
holds the psychographic models (`Draft 4`: client internal states, trust formation,
decision drivers). Possessing a model of someone's fear is precisely what makes
exploiting it possible. The models exist to **serve alignment, never to engineer
pressure.** This is consistent with the agency constitution's own integrity rules and
with Sales (05)'s risk/trust governance.

## 5. The Acquisition State Machines

Two runtimes. They are not the same length, the same shape, or the same speed.

**Client Acquisition — 12 states** (`Draft 8`):
`DISCOVER → RESEARCH → QUALIFY → POSITION → ENGAGE → NURTURE → VALIDATE → NEGOTIATE →
CONVERT → ONBOARD → RETAIN → EXPAND`

**Partner Acquisition — 11 states** (`Draft 8`):
`ECOSYSTEM MAP → STRATEGIC FIT → INCENTIVE ALIGNMENT → TRUST DEVELOPMENT → CAPABILITY
VALIDATION → COLLABORATION TEST → STRUCTURE DESIGN → FORMALIZATION → ACTIVATION →
EXPANSION → COMPOUNDING`

> *"Partner acquisition is slower but more compounding. The runtime MUST understand
> this difference."*

**⚠️ Three partner lifecycles exist and are not reconciled — see §9.1.** For **agency-side**
partner work, the **`CRM_SCHEMA.md` 11-stage pipeline is authoritative**, because it is
live. `Draft 8`'s 11 states and `Draft 6`'s 7-stage funnel are used as *reasoning
lenses*, not as competing pipelines.

## 6. Psychographic Law

**Client psychology is problem-driven.** Drivers: pain relief, speed, certainty, ROI
clarity. Internal states (`Draft 4`): **Desperate/Reactive · Aware/Evaluating ·
Strategic/Growth-Oriented** — each needs different pacing and different proof.

**Partner psychology is opportunity-driven.** Drivers: leverage, alignment, **control
retention**, strategic upside.

A partner is not a slow client. A client is not a small partner. §3 is the law; this is
how it shows up in a conversation.

## 7. Authority & Risk

Risk classes are the agency constitution's **Class 0–4** scheme (§5). It is supreme;
this department maps onto it, never beside it.

| Work | Class | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Psychographic profiling, partner sourcing/scoring (agency-side) | **1** | Internal analysis, no external commitment |
| Client-facing diagnosis, system/CRM/ecosystem design | **2** | Shapes a real client deliverable and client commitments |
| Partner enablement (toolkits, portals, partner-facing commitments) | **2** | Creates expectations in a third party |
| Anything creating a **contractual or economic** obligation — revenue splits, partnership agreements, rev-share terms, incentive structures | **3+** | Money and legal exposure; **human sign-off required, no exceptions** |

**Advisory-first.** Every agent in this department recommends and logs. **No agent
contacts a partner, sends a proposal, signs a term, or writes to a client's system.**

**Escalation thresholds** (`Draft 8`, `Draft 11` — carried as **illustrative starting
points**, not validated live thresholds; see §9.4):
- Acquisition confidence **< 85%** → escalate
- Trust degradation probability **> 40%** → escalate
- **Reputation exposure detected** → escalate
- **Contractual ambiguity** → escalate

Escalation format (`Draft 8`): **1. Problem · 2. Risk · 3. Blocker · 4. Suggested
Resolution · 5. Required Approval.**

Revenue-share terms are **governed by Legal (10)** and reconciled with Finance (09)
(`CRM_SCHEMA.md` Partner object).

## 8. Execution Discipline

**Deterministic language.** `Draft 8` requires operational grammar:
> Use: **MUST · MUST NOT · REQUIRED · ESCALATE · VALIDATE · VERIFY · CLASSIFY ·
> QUALIFY · IF/THEN · BEFORE/AFTER.**
> Avoid: *try, maybe, possibly* — *"Ambiguity destroys operational reliability."*

**Memory is append-only.**
> **"CRM logs MUST append, NEVER overwrite."** (`Drafts 8`, `13`)

This matches the runtime's JSONL memory contract (`arika-runtime/DESIGN.md`) — append,
never rewrite. Institutional relationship memory is the department's compounding asset:
> *"The CRM must remember every interaction, every objection, every stakeholder, every
> scope change, every promise… Without this, institutional intelligence collapses."*

**Bad partners are worse than no partners.**
> *"Bad partners: waste time, damage reputation, create operational chaos, dilute
> positioning."* Rejection is a first-class outcome. `<30` score = reject, and that is a
> success of the system, not a failure of it.

## 9. Genuine Gaps — flagged, not filled

**9.1 — Three unreconciled partner lifecycles.** `CRM_SCHEMA.md`'s 11-stage pipeline
(live), `Draft 8`'s 11 partner states, and `Draft 6`'s 7-stage funnel are three
different models with different names and counts. §5 makes CRM_SCHEMA authoritative for
agency-side work as an operating decision; **the three have not been genuinely
reconciled.** Owner decision.

**9.2 — CPAROS tier pricing is illustrative, not adopted.** `Draft 2`'s ladder — AFS
($2K–$10K setup / $1K–$5K retainer), AGE ($10K–$50K / $5K–$25K + performance), ADN
($50K–$250K+ / $25K–$100K+ + 2–10% rev share) — and its outcome projections
(+100–2000% pipeline, 10–80% CAC reduction) are **Claude-generated output, never
validated against a real engagement.** The **tier structure** is registered in Offer
(02); **the numbers are not agency pricing.** Same status as `Draft 6`'s explicitly
labelled *"Example Split"* (Distribution 20–40%, Fulfillment 40–70%, Strategic
Connector 10–20%). Pricing authority is Offer (02)'s `offer-pricing-floor-analyst`.
`CLAUDE.md` forbids inventing pricing.

**9.3 — No CRM object exists for client-facing acquisition work.** `CRM_SCHEMA.md`'s
`Partner` is **agency-side only** — it sources Opportunities *for Arika*. Nothing
represents *"we are building an acquisition system **for** client X."* That engagement
currently has to live as an Operations (08) `Project`. Whether client-side acquisition
assets need their own schema is unresolved.

**9.4 — Every threshold is illustrative.** 85% confidence, 40% trust degradation, the
45+/30–44/<30 partner score bands, the 5-dimension /10 scoring — all from draft
examples, **none validated by a real partnership.** Use as starting points; do not
report them as measured.

**9.5 — Zero real partners, clients, or deals exist.** No partner names, no engagements,
no performance data anywhere in the corpus (`CLIENTPARTNER_OS.md` §2). Every agent in
this department must reason about a real input or say it has none. **Nothing here is
evidence of a working system.**

**9.6 — `Drafts 8` and `12` are largely superseded.** Their acquisition doctrine (the
Trust Doctrine, the core distinction, the state machines, escalation) is **real and
adopted above**. But their bulk is meta-architecture for *how to prompt Claude Code /
Claude Coworker* — a 12-layer `/constitution /governance /agents /memory /runtime`
repository spec. **That system now exists**: `arika-runtime/` is the executor,
`AGENCY_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md` is the constitution, `GLOBAL_OS.md` is the
instruction hierarchy, and the JSONL streams are the memory layer. `Draft 8` predicted
the repo it now lives in; its prompt-design content should not be rebuilt.
