**$1,000,000 per month** — every month, non-negotiable.

| Period | Target | Derivation |

| Monthly | $1,000,000 | Owner-stated core target |

| Yearly | $12,000,000 | $1M × 12 (derived) |

**Weekly/quarterly/half-year/yearly figures above are arithmetic derivations from the owner's stated monthly/daily numbers, not separately confirmed by the owner** — flagged per the no-silent-invention rule. They're simple multiplication, not invented figures, but worth a quick owner confirmation pass since real operating cadence (e.g. weekends, holiday slowdowns) could mean these aren't simply linear.

## The Deal-Count Logic (how $35K/day actually gets hit)

The owner gave two equivalent paths to the daily target, both non-negotiable thresholds:

- **Path A:** 7 closed offers or value quotations per day, each ≥ $5,000
- **Path B:** 5 closed deals per day, each ≥ $7,000

**Revenue is explicitly modular, not fixed to one offer type.** The owner's own example: this doesn't require the same $5K/$7K deal every time — it could be 10 smaller engagements (e.g. website builds, strategic advisory) that collectively hit $30K-$35K/day. The cognitive system needs to map *which* combinations of offers reach the target, not assume one repeatable deal shape.

## The 7 Cognitive Calendars

The business operates as 7 synchronized intelligence calendars, not isolated tracking sheets — they inform each other:

| Calendar | Function |
|---|---|
| Revenue Calendar | Daily revenue targets and deal flow (the "heartbeat") |
| Pipeline Probability Calendar | Prospect movement, deal size × close probability × timeline × friction/urgency/strategic-value scoring |
| Operational Calendar | Execution and delivery capacity — prevents overselling past fulfillment ability |
| Cash Flow Calendar | Timing of money (revenue closed ≠ cash collected) — invoice timing, payment schedules, expenses |
| Capacity Calendar | Operational bandwidth (referenced as both its own calendar and folded into the Operational Calendar across the two source passes — not fully disambiguated by the owner; treat as one functional concern) |
| Opportunity Calendar | Which deals to pursue vs. ignore — ROI vs. effort, ranked by revenue speed, margin, strategic value, recurring potential, referral potential, AI leverage, operational complexity |
| Strategic Calendar | Long-term scaling — expansion timing, market entry, offer evolution, system upgrades |

### Pipeline scoring variables (Pipeline Probability Calendar)

Every deal gets scored on: Deal Size, Close Probability, Decision Timeline, Friction Score (obstacles), Urgency Score (buying pressure), Strategic Value (long-term importance), Upsell Potential.

### Cash-flow discipline (Cash Flow Calendar)

Explicit principle: **revenue closed is not cash collected.** A business might close $300K in a period but only collect $40K that week. The calendar must separately track invoice timing, payment schedules, retainers, delayed payments, contractor costs, software expenses, taxes, and emergency reserve — this is "survival intelligence," distinct from the Revenue Calendar's close-tracking.

## The Ascension Engine (how offers ladder toward the target)

Reuses the same 4-stage ascension path already named in `AGENCY_VISION.md` (Audit → Infrastructure → Optimization → Embedded Partnership → Enterprise Transformation), applied here to deal-velocity-vs-deal-value tradeoffs:

| Stage | Offer type | Volume | Close speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Audits, diagnostics, AI assessments, quick wins | High volume | Fast close, immediate cash |
| 2 | Infrastructure implementation (CRM, outbound, automation, funnels) | Medium volume | Medium close (14-30 days) |
| 3 | Retainers, optimization, advisory, revenue operations | Lower volume, recurring | Higher trust required |
| 4 | Embedded partnerships, enterprise transformation, AI enablement office, equity participation | Lowest volume | Strategic, longest cycle, highest value |

This is the same logic already captured in `02_Offer/OFFER_OS.md`'s Capability Registry (audit-as-entry-offer pattern) and now has a real numeric backbone connecting it to the $1M/month target.

## Operating Philosophy: Revenue Mathematics, Not Activity Tracking

The owner's own framing, preserved verbatim because it's a real operating principle, not paraphrase: *"We are not doing generic work. We are actually doing the actual work. And we are not going to have things that are not being paid."* Every activity must answer:

| Question | Why |
|---|---|
| Does this create revenue? | Survival |
| How much revenue? | Prioritization |
| How fast? | Cash flow |
| What probability of closing? | Forecasting |
| What operational load? | Scalability |
| What retention potential? | Stability |
| What upsell path? | Expansion |

Named anti-patterns to actively avoid (owner's own list): no meaningless meetings, no vanity tasks, no random content, no fake productivity, no outreach without qualification, no fulfillment without monetization logic. The term the source material uses for this discipline: **"Revenue-Oriented Cognitive Business Operations (ROCBO)."**
