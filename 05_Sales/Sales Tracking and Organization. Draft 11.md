How should everything be tracked and organized in sales 

If your sales system isn’t tracked and organized properly, even the best strategy collapses into guesswork. What you want is a system where **nothing relies on memory, everything is visible, and performance is measurable in real time**.

Here’s how to structure it at an elite level:

**1\. Central Source of Truth (Your Sales Brain)**

You need one place where all activity lives—typically a CRM like Salesforce CRM, HubSpot CRM, or Pipedrive.

This is non-negotiable.

Everything goes in:

  * Leads 
  * Contacts 
  * Deals 
  * Activities (calls, emails, meetings) 
  * Notes & objections 
  * Follow-ups 



**Rule:** If it’s not in the CRM, it didn’t happen.

**2\. Pipeline Structure (Visual Control)**

Your pipeline should mirror your actual sales process, not a generic template.

Typical stages:

  1. Lead captured 
  2. Qualified 
  3. Discovery done 
  4. Proposal sent 
  5. Negotiation 
  6. Closed (Won/Lost) 



Each stage must have:

  * Clear **entry criteria**
  * Clear **exit criteria**



This removes ambiguity and keeps deals moving.

**3\. KPI Tracking (System Pulse)**

Track metrics at **three levels** :

**Activity KPIs (Input)**

  * Calls made 
  * Emails sent 
  * Meetings booked 
  * Follow-ups completed 



**Conversion KPIs (Efficiency)**

  * Lead → Qualified % 
  * Qualified → Proposal % 
  * Proposal → Close % 



**Revenue KPIs (Output)**

  * Deals closed 
  * Revenue generated 
  * Average deal size 
  * Sales cycle length 



This creates a cause-effect chain:  
**Activity → Conversion → Revenue**

**4\. Sales Dashboard (Real-Time Visibility)**

Every sales leader and rep should see:

  * Pipeline value 
  * Deals per stage 
  * Conversion rates 
  * Individual performance 



Use dashboards inside tools like HubSpot CRM or Salesforce CRM.

The goal:  
No meetings needed to “figure out what’s going on.”

**5\. Daily & Weekly Cadence (Execution Rhythm)**

**Daily:**

  * Log all activities 
  * Update deal stages 
  * Set next actions for every deal 



**Weekly:**

  * Pipeline review (what’s stuck?) 
  * KPI review (where are leaks?) 
  * Forecast update 



**Every 14 Days (your momentum cycle idea):**

  * Diagnose bottlenecks 
  * Adjust scripts/approach 
  * Re-align with marketing 



**6\. Deal-Level Tracking (Micro Control)**

Each deal should have:

  * Decision-makers identified 
  * Pain points clearly defined 
  * Budget clarity 
  * Timeline 
  * Next step scheduled (always) 



If a deal has no next step → it’s already dead.

**7\. Lead Source Tracking (Marketing Alignment)**

Track exactly where leads come from:

  * Ads 
  * Referrals 
  * Organic 
  * Outreach 



This connects sales performance to marketing effectiveness.

Without this, you can’t scale what works.

**8\. Documentation & Playbooks (System Memory)**

Track and document:

  * Winning scripts 
  * Objection handling 
  * Closing techniques 
  * Industry-specific approaches 



Your system should get **smarter over time** , not reset every month.

**9\. Automation Layer (Efficiency Engine)**

Automate:

  * Follow-up reminders 
  * Email sequences 
  * Lead assignment 
  * Reporting 



Tools like Zapier or built-in CRM automation help remove manual friction.

**10\. Accountability System (Performance Pressure)**

Every rep should clearly know:

  * Their targets 
  * Their KPIs 
  * Their gaps 



And you track:

  * Who is consistent 
  * Who is falling behind 
  * Where coaching is needed
