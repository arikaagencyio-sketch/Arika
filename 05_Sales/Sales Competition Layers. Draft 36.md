Even sales has competitiors right

Yes—and not just in the obvious way.

Sales doesn’t only compete with _other sellers_. It competes across multiple layers, and if you miss that, you’ll misdiagnose why deals stall or die.

Here’s the real picture:

**1\. Direct competitors (obvious layer)**

These are the companies offering similar solutions.

  * Same product category 
  * Same target customer 
  * Similar pricing or positioning 



This is where most sales teams focus—but it’s actually the **least dangerous layer** , because it’s visible.

**2\. Indirect competitors (alternative solutions)**

These solve the same problem differently.

  * Different approach, same outcome 
  * Could be cheaper, faster, or simpler 
  * Sometimes not even in your “industry” 



Example:  
Your SaaS vs hiring a freelancer vs doing nothing with Excel.

**3\. Internal competition (most underestimated)**

This is where deals really get killed.

You’re competing against:

  * Budget constraints 
  * Internal politics 
  * Other projects 
  * Decision-maker priorities 



This means your real competitor might be:

“Let’s postpone this to next quarter.”

**4\. Status quo (the strongest competitor)**

This is the default enemy in almost every deal.

  * “What we have works fine” 
  * “It’s not urgent” 
  * “We’ll think about it” 



Doing nothing is often safer for the buyer than choosing you.

**5\. Attention competition**

You’re not just selling a product—you’re competing for **focus**.

  * Emails vs meetings vs internal fires 
  * Other vendors pitching at the same time 
  * Executive bandwidth 



If you don’t win attention, you never even enter the real competition.

**6\. Trust competition**

Even if you’re the best option, buyers ask:

  * Do we trust you? 
  * Do we trust your company? 
  * Will this actually work? 



So you’re competing against:

  * Reputation 
  * Past experiences 
  * Perceived risk 



**What this means for your sales system**

If your system only prepares for “competitors,” it’s incomplete.

A high-level sales system must map:

  * **Market layer** → direct + indirect competitors 
  * **Account layer** → internal stakeholders + politics 
  * **Psychological layer** → fear, risk, inertia 
  * **Timing layer** → urgency vs delay 
  * **Attention layer** → access to decision-makers 



**Strategic shift (this is the key insight)**

Sales is not:

“Why us vs them?”

It is:

“Why change at all—and why now—with us?”

If you don’t win _that_ , you’re not even in the real game.
