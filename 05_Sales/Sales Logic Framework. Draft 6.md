What logic should apply in sales

Sales without logic is just guesswork. The difference between average and elite performance is having a _clear decision-making framework_ behind every action.

Here’s the kind of logic that should guide sales—practical, grounded, and actually usable:

**1\. Value Logic (Why should they care?)**

Every sale must answer:

  * Does this solve a real, painful problem? 
  * Is the value **greater than the cost (money, time, risk)?**



If your offer doesn’t clearly improve the buyer’s situation, no script or tactic will save it.

👉 Core rule:  
**Perceived Value > Perceived Cost = Sale moves forward**

**2\. Customer Logic (Why _this_ buyer?)**

Not everyone is your customer. Strong sales logic filters first.

Ask:

  * Do they have the problem? 
  * Do they feel the urgency? 
  * Do they have the ability to pay? 



👉 If any of these are missing, you’re pushing instead of selling.

**3\. Timing Logic (Why now?)**

Deals don’t close because of interest—they close because of **timing pressure**.

  * Is there a deadline? 
  * Is there a consequence of waiting? 
  * Is something triggering change? 



👉 No urgency = no decision.

**4\. Trust Logic (Why you?)**

People don’t buy the best option. They buy the **most trusted option**.

Trust comes from:

  * Credibility (proof, results, testimonials) 
  * Consistency (clear, confident communication) 
  * Understanding (you “get” their problem) 



👉 If trust is low, resistance is high.

**5\. Decision Logic (How do they decide?)**

Every buyer has an internal process:

  * Logical buyers → need data, ROI, comparisons 
  * Emotional buyers → need confidence, reassurance 
  * Group buyers → need alignment from others 



👉 Your job is to match their decision style—not force yours.

**6\. Friction Logic (What’s stopping them?)**

Most lost deals aren’t rejection—they’re friction.

Common friction:

  * Confusion 
  * Too many options 
  * Fear of risk 
  * Complicated process 



👉 Sales logic = **reduce friction, not increase persuasion**

**7\. Consistency Logic (Does everything align?)**

If your:

  * Messaging 
  * Pricing 
  * Positioning 
  * Delivery 



…don’t align, buyers hesitate.

👉 Example: Premium price + weak branding = doubt.

**8\. Follow-up Logic (Why continue?)**

Most sales happen after the first interaction.

Good follow-up logic:

  * Adds value (not just “checking in”) 
  * Addresses objections progressively 
  * Builds familiarity 



👉 No system = lost revenue.

**9\. Data Logic (What is actually working?)**

Sales isn’t feelings—it’s patterns.

Track:

  * Conversion rates 
  * Drop-off points 
  * Lead quality 



👉 Then ask every 14 days (as you mentioned earlier):

  * Where are we losing people? 
  * Why here specifically? 



**10\. Momentum Logic (Is the system compounding?)**

Sales success isn’t isolated wins—it’s repeatable flow.

You should always ask:

  * Is pipeline growing or shrinking? 
  * Are conversions improving? 
  * Are deals moving faster? 



👉 If not, something is broken upstream.

**Right person + real problem + clear value + trust + urgency − friction = closed deal**
