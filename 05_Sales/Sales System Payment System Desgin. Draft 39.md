How to pay the sales system and everyone involved

If you don’t design how people get paid **early** , your sales system will quietly break—misaligned incentives will outperform your strategy every time. So this isn’t just payroll; it’s **behavior design for revenue**.

Let’s structure it properly.

**1\. Start with the core principle:_What are you rewarding?_**

Every role in your sales system should be paid based on what they **directly control** :

  * If they **create demand** → reward pipeline creation 
  * If they **convert deals** → reward revenue closed 
  * If they **retain/expand clients** → reward lifetime value 
  * If they **enable others** → reward system efficiency 



If you mix these up, performance drops.

**2\. Build a layered compensation architecture**

**A. Revenue Drivers (Frontline Sales)**

**1\. Closers (Sales Reps / Account Executives)**

  * **Base salary** (stability) 
  * **Commission per deal closed**
  * Optional: accelerators (higher % after hitting target) 



Example:

  * Base: $500/month 
  * Commission: 10% per deal 
  * Accelerator: 15% after $10K revenue 



👉 This aligns directly with **cash in**

**2\. Setters / SDRs (Lead Qualification)**

  * **Base salary**
  * **Per qualified meeting bonus**
  * Optional: % of closed deal (small) 



Example:

  * $5 per qualified call 
  * 1–2% of deal closed 



👉 They are paid for **pipeline quality, not revenue**

**3\. Partnership / Channel Sales**

  * **Revenue share model**



Example:

  * 20–40% of deal value (depends on contribution) 



👉 Best for scaling without fixed costs

**B. Growth & Retention Layer**

**4\. Account Managers / Customer Success**

  * Base salary 
  * Bonus on: 
    * renewals 
    * upsells 
    * retention rate 



Example:

  * 5% upsell commission 
  * 90%+ retention bonus 



👉 They protect and expand revenue

**C. Marketing & Demand Generation**

**5\. Marketing Team**

Avoid paying purely on “leads”—that creates junk.

Instead:

  * Base salary 
  * Bonus tied to: 
    * **qualified pipeline value**
    * conversion rates 
    * cost per acquisition 



👉 Align marketing with **sales outcomes, not activity**

**D. Operations & Enablement**

**6\. Sales Ops / Systems / Analytics**

  * Fixed salary 
  * Performance bonus tied to: 
    * system efficiency (conversion improvements) 
    * reporting accuracy 
    * pipeline velocity 



👉 They don’t sell—but they multiply sales performance

**3\. Add system-wide incentives (critical for elite systems)**

**A. Team Overrides**

  * Small % shared across team when target is hit 



Example:

  * 2–5% bonus pool distributed across contributors 



👉 Prevents silos

**B. Milestone Bonuses**

  * Launch bonus 
  * Quarterly targets 
  * Strategic deals 



👉 Drives focus on big outcomes

**C. Profit-Based Bonus (Advanced)**

  * % of net profit shared with leadership layer 



👉 Aligns long-term thinking

**4\. Choose your payout model (cash flow matters)**

**Option 1: High Commission, Low Salary**

  * Good for early-stage 
  * Risk transferred to sales team 



**Option 2: Balanced (most common)**

  * Base + commission 



**Option 3: High Salary, Low Commission**

  * Good for complex enterprise sales 



**5\. Define payout timing (often overlooked)**

  * Commission paid on: 
    * **cash received** (safest) 
    * OR contract signed (riskier) 
  * Delay payments if: 
    * refunds occur 
    * client churns early 



👉 Protects your business from overpaying

**6\. Build a clear compensation formula**

Every role should have a simple equation:

**Earnings = Base + (Performance Metric × Rate) + Bonuses**

If it’s confusing, it won’t work.

**7\. Budget rule (non-negotiable)**

Your total sales compensation should typically be:

  * **10% – 30% of revenue**



If it’s higher:

  * you’re overpaying  
If it’s lower: 
  * you’ll struggle to attract performers 



**8\. Common mistakes to avoid**

  * Paying on **activity instead of outcomes**
  * Overcomplicating commission structures 
  * No cap or no structure (chaos) 
  * Not aligning marketing + sales incentives 
  * Paying before revenue is secure
