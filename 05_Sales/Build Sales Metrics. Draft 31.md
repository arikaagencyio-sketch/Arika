**1) Building Financial Metrics in Sales (the right way)**

Start with a simple truth:  
Sales metrics are not about activity—they’re about **revenue mechanics and predictability**.

**A. Core Revenue Structure (Foundation Layer)**

You build from the bottom up:

**Revenue Equation**

At its simplest:

Revenue = Number of Deals × Average Deal Size

But for a real system:

Revenue = Leads × Conversion Rate × Average Contract Value (ACV)

Now expand it into a **full funnel financial model** :

Revenue =   
(Leads → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Closed Won)  
× Conversion Rates at each stage  
× ACV

This is your **financial spine**.

**B. Key Financial Metrics in Sales**

These are non-negotiable if you want a high-performance system:

**1\. ACV (Average Contract Value)**

  * Average revenue per deal (annualized) 
  * Drives deal quality and targeting strategy 



**2\. ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**

  * Total recurring revenue normalized to a year 
  * This is your **north star for scale**



**ARR Formula**

**3\. Pipeline Value**

Pipeline = Σ (Deal Value × Probability of Closing)

This is how you forecast reality—not hope.

**4\. Win Rate**

Win Rate = Closed Won Deals / Total Opportunities

Tells you execution quality.

**5\. Sales Cycle Length**

  * Time from first touch → close 
  * Impacts cash flow timing 



**6\. Revenue Expansion Metrics**

  * Upsell 
  * Cross-sell 
  * Net Revenue Retention (NRR) 



**C. Financial Layering (Advanced System Thinking)**

A serious sales system breaks revenue into:

  * **New Business ARR**
  * **Expansion ARR**
  * **Renewal ARR**
  * **Churn (negative ARR)**



So:

Net ARR = New + Expansion + Renewals – Churn

That’s how real operators see revenue—not just “sales closed.”

**3) Why ARR in Sales ≠ ARR in Marketing**

This is a critical distinction most teams mess up.

**Marketing View of ARR**

  * Predictive / modeled 
  * Based on funnel assumptions: 
    * Leads × conversion × pricing 
  * Often **theoretical or projected ARR**



**Sales View of ARR**

  * **Contracted reality**
  * Based on: 
    * Signed deals 
    * Negotiated terms 
    * Actual close dates 



**The Core Difference**

**Aspect**| **Marketing**| **Sales**  
---|---|---  
ARR Type| Projected| Realized  
Basis| Funnel assumptions| Closed deals  
Timing| Immediate estimates| Delayed (sales cycle)  
Accuracy| Probabilistic| Contractual  
  
**Where the tension comes from**

Marketing says:

“We generated enough pipeline for $5M ARR”

Sales says:

“We only closed $2M ARR”

The gap =

  * Conversion inefficiencies 
  * Deal quality issues 
  * Sales execution problems 
  * Or bad targeting from marketing
