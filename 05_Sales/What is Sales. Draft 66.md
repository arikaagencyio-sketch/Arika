Lets talk about sales what is sales

Sales is the process of helping someone decide to buy a product or service that solves a problem or meets a need. At its core, it’s not just about “pushing” something—it’s about understanding people and creating value.

Think of it this way:  
If someone is thirsty and you offer them water, that’s sales. You’re connecting a need (thirst) with a solution (water).

**What sales really involves**

  * **Understanding needs** – figuring out what the customer actually wants or struggles with 
  * **Communicating value** – explaining how your product/service helps them 
  * **Building trust** – people buy from those they believe 
  * **Closing the deal** – guiding them to make a decision 



**Simple example**

A phone seller isn’t just selling a phone—they’re selling:

  * Better communication 
  * Social connection 
  * Productivity 
  * Status or lifestyle 



**Types of sales**

  * **Retail sales** – selling directly in shops 
  * **B2B (business-to-business)** – selling to companies 
  * **Online sales** – e-commerce, social media, etc. 
  * **Service sales** – like insurance, consulting, or training 



**Key idea**

Good sales is **problem-solving + communication** , not pressure.

how many types of sales do we have

There isn’t one fixed number—because sales can be categorized in different ways—but in practice, most people group sales into **5–8 main types**. Here are the most widely recognized ones, explained simply:

**1\. B2C (Business-to-Consumer)**

Selling directly to individual customers.  
Example: A shop selling clothes or phones.

**2\. B2B (Business-to-Business)**

Selling from one business to another.  
Example: A company selling software or office supplies to another company.

**3\. Inside Sales**

Selling remotely (phone, email, WhatsApp, Zoom).  
No physical meeting required.

**4\. Outside Sales**

Face-to-face selling.  
Example: A salesperson visiting clients or meeting in person.

**5\. Retail Sales**

Selling in a physical store directly to walk-in customers.

**6\. E-commerce / Online Sales**

Selling through websites or platforms like social media.

**7\. Consultative Sales**

You act more like an advisor—understanding the customer deeply and recommending the best solution.

**8\. Direct Sales**

Selling straight to the customer without a middleman.  
Example: door-to-door or personal selling.

**Simple way to remember**

You can group them into 3 big buckets:

  * **Who you sell to** → B2C, B2B 
  * **How you sell** → Inside, Outside, Online 
  * **Style of selling** → Consultative, Direct
