## Skill Specs

### Sales Strategy Governance

Procedure:

1. Clarify mission, objective, risk, and time horizon.
2. Collect evidence from market, RevOps, offer, and execution agents.
3. Separate strategy from tactics and rank tradeoffs.
4. Define owner, cadence, decision threshold, and review date.
5. Escalate pricing, legal, finance, hiring, and strategic shifts.

Standard output: strategic action brief, evidence, assumptions, decision owner, approval status, review cadence.

### Sales Market Intelligence

Procedure:

1. Identify the market question and decision it supports.
2. Gather source evidence and distinguish facts from inference.
3. Map competitors, segments, buyer behavior, and timing signals.
4. Produce a concise intelligence brief with confidence and next action.
5. Update memory when a market assumption becomes a rule.

Standard output: market brief, ICP implications, competitor notes, timing signals, confidence, next action.

### Sales Customer Psychology

Procedure:

1. Start from buyer evidence, not stereotypes.
2. Map pain, desired outcome, risk perception, authority, and urgency.
3. Identify objections as uncertainty, trust gap, priority conflict, or economics.
4. Recommend ethical framing that clarifies, not manipulates.
5. Escalate sensitive or unsupported psychological claims.

Standard output: buyer psychology map, objection diagnosis, trust gap, decision-chain notes, ethical framing.

### Sales Offer Architecture

Procedure:

1. Start with ICP, pain, desired outcome, proof, and delivery capacity.
2. Define the outcome, mechanism, value metric, and risk reducer.
3. Map pricing and packaging assumptions separately from claims.
4. Create messaging that is specific and evidence-aware.
5. Escalate promises, guarantees, legal claims, or pricing decisions.

Standard output: offer map, value proposition, pricing logic, risk reducers, proof needs, approval notes.

### Sales Demand Generation

Procedure:

1. Define ICP, channel, offer, message, and quality target.
2. Choose channel actions based on buyer behavior and source quality.
3. Create sequence, campaign, or partner plan with measurement.
4. Feed lead quality back to qualification and RevOps.
5. Escalate spend, compliance, brand risk, or high-volume outreach.

Standard output: channel plan, campaign hypothesis, source quality metric, sequence, experiment rule.

### Sales Audience Community

Procedure:

1. Clarify audience identity, current belief, and desired movement.
2. Translate expertise into educational content and trust signals.
3. Design narrative sequence before direct conversion ask.
4. Connect content to offer and lead capture paths.
5. Escalate sensitive claims, reputation risk, or public positioning changes.

Standard output: audience strategy, content map, trust sequence, authority assets, community activation.

### Sales Qualification Discovery

Procedure:

1. Define qualification criteria and disqualification rules.
2. Gather fit, urgency, budget, authority, timing, and pain evidence.
3. Ask before prescribing; separate curiosity from closing pressure.
4. Return score, rationale, next step, and missing data.
5. Escalate strategic accounts or unclear authority.

Standard output: qualification score, discovery brief, next step, missing evidence, disqualification rule.

### Sales Execution Closing

Procedure:

1. Read discovery and buyer psychology context first.
2. Diagnose the real source of friction before writing responses.
3. Align offer, value, risk reducer, stakeholder path, and timing.
4. Produce talk track, close plan, and next-step sequence.
5. Escalate legal terms, discounts, guarantees, and relationship-sensitive moves.

Standard output: call plan, objection map, negotiation guardrails, close path, deal risk.

### Sales Follow-Up Recovery

Procedure:

1. Identify last contact, promise history, buyer state, and next friction.
2. Choose follow-up type: value, clarity, timing, proof, or close-the-loop.
3. Keep messages specific and respectful.
4. Attach every open loop to owner, date, and next action.
5. Escalate repeated follow-up or negative sentiment risk.

Standard output: follow-up sequence, recovery diagnosis, CRM next step, owner, due date.

### Sales Customer Success Expansion

Procedure:

1. Translate sales promises into onboarding commitments.
2. Track adoption, value realization, health, risk, and renewal timing.
3. Identify expansion triggers only after value evidence exists.
4. Create account plan with risks, opportunities, and next actions.
5. Escalate churn risk, scope conflict, terms, or delivery gaps.

Standard output: onboarding plan, customer health note, expansion trigger, renewal path, risk note.

### Sales RevOps Performance

Procedure:

1. Define the decision each metric or field supports.
2. Map CRM stages, fields, owners, exit criteria, and hygiene rules.
3. Build forecasts with assumptions and confidence levels.
4. Identify data quality risks before conclusions.
5. Escalate metric changes, compensation impact, destructive data changes, and official forecasts.

Standard output: CRM spec, KPI dictionary, dashboard view, forecast note, automation backlog.

### Sales Power Partnership

Procedure:

1. Map formal authority, informal influence, blockers, and incentives.
2. Separate ethical influence from manipulation.
3. Design access paths and partner value exchange.
4. Package political risk and next relationship move.
5. Escalate sensitive relationship moves and partnership economics.

Standard output: power map, access strategy, partner structure, political risk, next relationship action.

### Sales Risk Governance

Procedure:

1. Classify risk before execution.
2. Check legal, trust, privacy, finance, and customer-facing implications.
3. Define approval owner and evidence required.
4. Record decision lineage and unresolved risk.
5. Block action until approval when risk is high or critical.

Standard output: risk class, approval path, evidence, blocked actions, audit note.

### Sales Enablement Playbooks

Procedure:

1. Start from a real workflow or skill gap.
2. Define owner, trigger, steps, inputs, outputs, and quality bar.
3. Create versioned playbook or SOP with usage context.
4. Connect asset to agent, CRM stage, and review cadence.
5. Escalate official script or SOP approval.

Standard output: SOP, playbook, script, training drill, certification check.

### Sales AI Orchestration

Procedure:

1. Classify intent, risk, and needed agent route.
2. Use the smallest capable agent path.
3. Package context, constraints, and expected output.
4. Require human approval for sensitive or irreversible actions.
5. Update memory when a prompt, rule, or decision should persist.

Standard output: agent route, handoff packet, prompt package, approval need, memory update.

### Sales Planning Workflows

Procedure:

1. Define objective, deliverable, owner, risk, and deadline.
2. Break work into sequenced tasks with dependencies.
3. Assign agents and expected outputs.
4. Define verification before execution starts.
5. Escalate unclear scope or conflicting priorities.

Standard output: execution plan, task sequence, dependency map, owner list, verification plan.

### Sales Tool Use Integration

Procedure:

1. Confirm tool, data target, permission, and expected output.
2. Retrieve or act only within approved boundary.
3. Record inputs, outputs, errors, and side effects.
4. Do not write, delete, send, or change external systems without approval.
5. Escalate sensitive data, unclear access, or irreversible action.

Standard output: tool result, data extract, audit note, error report, next step.

### Sales Reflection Quality

Procedure:

1. Check task alignment, evidence, assumptions, and completeness.
2. Find contradictions, missing data, and unsupported claims.
3. Assign confidence with reasons.
4. Recommend corrections or escalation.
5. Capture learning for future agent, skill, SOP, or routing updates.

Standard output: validation report, confidence score, corrections, contradiction log, learning update.

## Universal Skill Guardrails

- Preserve source lineage when using the sales corpus.
- Separate evidence from inference.
- Do not invent customer, market, CRM, financial, or legal facts.
- Escalate high-risk, external, legal, financial, destructive, or relationship-sensitive actions.
