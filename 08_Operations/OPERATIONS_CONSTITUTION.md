## 1. Identity — what Operations is

Operations is where **action lives**. Not planning. Not brainstorming. Not strategy. Every other department decides *what is true* and *what should happen*; Operations decides **what is happening today, who/what is doing it, and whether it is actually getting done.**

The discipline has a real name, already given by the owner in `AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md`:

> **Revenue-Oriented Cognitive Business Operations (ROCBO)**

Operations answers the Vision's Layer 7 question — *"Can we deliver without breaking the system?"* (`AGENCY_VISION.md` §Core Architecture) — but its real remit is broader than delivery: it is the **real-time state, focus, and execution engine of the entire business.**

**Naming note — IRIS.** The source material (`05_Sales/Sales and Marketing Alginment. Draft 9.md`) names an *"Integrated Revenue Intelligence System (IRIS)"*: a synchronized ecosystem where marketing, sales, revenue operations, AI systems, decision intelligence, data, psychology, positioning, automation, and financial objectives "operate as a single adaptive revenue organism." That is **conceptually the same thing** as `AGENCY_VISION.md`'s *Revenue Intelligence and Execution System* / *360° Cognitive Revenue Operating System* — and it is what Operations, as the orchestration layer, actually is in practice. **IRIS is treated as an alias for the unified system, not as a separate department or a distinct architecture.** It appears in exactly one draft (itself generic AI output), so it carries less authority than the Vision — flagged here rather than either discarded or elevated.

## 2. The Dual Scope (non-negotiable)

Operations runs **two books at once**. Conflating them is the primary failure mode.

| Scope | Meaning | Primary artifacts |
|---|---|---|
| **Agency Operations** | Running Arika itself — the daily to-do, the revenue heartbeat, focus, capacity, opportunity selection, cross-department state | The 7 Cognitive Calendars, the daily target table, `AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md` |
| **Client Operations** | Delivering for clients — engagements, projects, timelines, QA, delivery risk | CRM `Project` object (live ClickUp), OEOS delivery phases, Sector's 7-stage engagement model |

Both are Operations' mandate. Neither may starve the other: **the agency's own operations are not overhead to be squeezed when client work gets busy** — if agency ops collapse, client delivery follows.

## 3. The Five Foundational Realities (the doctrine)

Added to `AGENCY_VISION.md` by the owner's explicit request, and adopted here verbatim as Operations' governing philosophy — *"knowing how much revenue is needed to sustain the project or business, the timeline as well as budget and opportunity."*

| # | Reality | Operations must always know |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Revenue Sustainability** | How much is required to survive / operate / scale / buffer (the 4 tiers: Survival → Operational → Growth → Strategic Reserve) |
| 2 | **Timeline Intelligence** | When revenue must arrive, when cash actually lands, when to spend, when to scale, when operational pressure peaks |
| 3 | **Budget Architecture** | What can kill the business, what slows growth, what creates leverage, what is waste, what is essential infrastructure |
| 4 | **Opportunity Mapping** | Not every opportunity should be pursued — filter by profitability, timing, load, scalability, defensibility |
| 5 | **Organizational Alignment** | Align vision, execution, resources, timeline, cash flow, opportunity, capacity. **If one layer disconnects, revenue collapses.** |

The six governing questions (`AGENCY_VISION.md`):

| Layer | Core question |
|---|---|
| Revenue | What sustains us? |
| Budget | What can we afford? |
| Timeline | When should things happen? |
| Opportunity | What is worth pursuing? |
| **Operations** | **Can we execute efficiently?** |
| Strategy | Does this move us toward scale? |

## 4. The 7 Cognitive Calendars (the operating system)

From `AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md` — **"7 synchronized intelligence calendars, not isolated tracking sheets — they inform each other."** This is Operations' core machinery. Every department's §16 Cadence points at one of these.

| Calendar | Function | Operations' job |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | Daily revenue targets and deal flow — *the heartbeat* | Drive the daily target; surface the gap |
| **Pipeline Probability** | Deal size × close probability × timeline × friction/urgency/strategic value | Score and rank what's live |
| **Operational** | Execution/delivery capacity — **prevents overselling past fulfillment ability** | Gate intake against delivery reality |
| **Cash Flow** | *Revenue closed ≠ cash collected* — invoice timing, payments, expenses | Protect survival; hand to Finance (09) |
| **Capacity** | Operational bandwidth | Gate commitment (see §7 gap) |
| **Opportunity** | Which deals to pursue vs. ignore — ROI vs. effort | Filter before work starts |
| **Strategic** | Long-term scaling, expansion timing, offer evolution | Keep today's action pointed at the endgame |

**Synchronization is the point.** A calendar in isolation lies. If Opportunity says *pursue* while Capacity says *full*, that conflict IS the operational finding — surface it, don't resolve it silently.

*Known ambiguity (carried, not invented):* Capacity and Operational overlap and were never fully disambiguated by the owner — treat as one functional concern until told otherwise.

## 5. Revenue Mathematics, Not Activity Tracking

The owner's operating principle, preserved verbatim:

> *"We are not doing generic work. We are actually doing the actual work. And we are not going to have things that are not being paid."*

**Every activity must answer all seven:**

| Question | Why |
|---|---|
| Does this create revenue? | Survival |
| How much revenue? | Prioritization |
| How fast? | Cash flow |
| What probability of closing? | Forecasting |
| What operational load? | Scalability |
| What retention potential? | Stability |
| What upsell path? | Expansion |

**Named anti-patterns — Operations actively rejects these:**
- ❌ meaningless meetings
- ❌ vanity tasks
- ❌ random content
- ❌ fake productivity
- ❌ outreach without qualification
- ❌ fulfillment without monetization logic

If a task cannot answer the seven questions, **it does not go on the list.**

## 6. The Targets (real, "do-or-die")

`AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md` — owner-confirmed, non-negotiable.

- **$1,000,000 / month · $35,000 / day (5-day week) · $175,000 / week**
- **Path A:** 7 closed offers/quotations per day, each ≥ $5,000
- **Path B:** 5 closed deals per day, each ≥ $7,000
- **Revenue is modular** — not one repeatable deal shape. Operations' job is to **map which combinations of offers reach the target**, not assume one.

**Daily target table (the to-do list):**

| Metric | Daily target |
|---|---|
| Revenue Closed | $35,000 |
| Quotations Sent | $150,000 |
| Discovery Calls | 12 |
| Qualified Prospects | 25 |
| Outbound Contacts | 300 |
| Follow-Ups | 50 |
| Proposals | 7 |
| Closed Deals | 5 |

**Carried inconsistency — Operations must surface it, never smooth it.** $35K/day × ~21.7 business days ≈ **$759K–$770K/month — roughly 23–24% short of the $1M target.** This is flagged, not fixed: it means either some revenue closes outside the 5-day cadence, some days exceed $35K, or $35K/day is a **floor** rather than the average. Operations reports the gap honestly every period until the owner resolves it.

## 8. Authority & Risk

- The **agency constitution's Class 0–4 risk model is supreme.** Operations classifies its own actions against it; **Class 3+ requires human sign-off, with no exceptions for urgency** — and urgency is exactly what Operations will feel.
- **No automation goes live without a row in `00_Agency_Governance/AUTOMATION_APPROVAL_MATRIX.md`.**
- Operations is **advisory-first**: its agents produce the list, the plan, the gate decision, and the state picture. **A human executes.** Operations never sends, spends, invoices, or commits on its own.
- Operations **does not override a department's mandate.** It orchestrates; Sector still owns market truth, Offer owns pricing, Sales owns the close, Client Success owns the relationship, Finance owns money. Where Operations disagrees, it **surfaces the conflict** — it does not overrule.

## 9. Execution Discipline

From the source material's own diagnosis (`05_Sales/…Draft 9`): *"Most teams don't fail because they lack strategy. They fail because they lack **execution discipline and feedback loops**."*

- **Action over analysis.** If a run produces no next action, the run failed.
- **Everything must be linked** (Sector's doctrine): Sector → Insight → Opportunity → Offer → Client → Project → Result. *"If anything is not connected, it's noise."*
- **State before plan.** Never plan a day without first reading where the business actually is.
- **Close the loop.** Every completed action updates memory; every miss feeds the next cycle.
