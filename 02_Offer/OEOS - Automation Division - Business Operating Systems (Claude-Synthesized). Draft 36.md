## Phase 2 — Positioning Architecture (synthesized)

"Operationalize growth" places this offer alongside, not against, offer #6 (AI Workflow Infrastructure) — both sit in the Automation Division and both target "operational chaos," but this offer is the **process-and-documentation layer** (the human/operational backbone: who does what, how, measured by what) while offer #6 is the **execution/automation layer** (the AI/agent systems that run inside that backbone). Worth flagging as a deliberate two-offer split within one division, useful for clients needing one without the other — e.g. a client with a working team but no documented process needs this offer before they need AI agents.

## Phase 3 — Backbone Architecture (synthesized)

**Core systems:** Process Mapping System, SOP Documentation System, Dashboard/KPI Engineering System, Team Infrastructure & Org-Design System, Reporting Cadence System.

**Immutable components:** process mapped and validated before it is documented (never write an SOP for an unvalidated process); single source-of-truth documentation repository; every KPI has a named, versioned owner (mirrors this repo's own KPI Dictionary discipline — `00_Agency_Governance/AGENCY_KPI_DICTIONARY.md`); quarterly process-audit cycle.

**Customization components:** process depth/complexity by department, dashboard tooling (existing BI stack vs. new build), org size (solo-founder vs. multi-team), industry-specific compliance documentation requirements.

## Phase 9 — Client Constraint System (synthesized)

Highest-risk archetypes: **Know-It-All** ("our process is fine, we don't need this documented") and **Quitter** — SOP adoption decays without enforcement, the same adoption-decay risk pattern already identified in offer #2's synthesized Phase 9 (`Draft 32`); directly cross-referenced rather than re-derived. Prevention mirrors offer #2's: a manager-led, non-optional adherence cadence built in from day one.

## Phase 10 — Quality Control System (synthesized)
