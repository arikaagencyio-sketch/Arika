## The OEOS Prompt (methodology, reusable for every future offer)

A 12-phase framework for engineering any offer as full operational infrastructure, not just a deliverable + price:

1. **Offer Identification** — identity, problem solved, transformation, target client, business stage served, revenue/operational/strategic impact, time-to-result, prerequisites, success criteria, unrealistic-expectations warning, constraints.
2. **Offer Positioning Architecture** — market/strategic/value position, competitive advantage, differentiators, value/risk-reduction/time-compression/revenue-leverage drivers, pricing justification, messaging layer (core promise, narrative, transformation, objections, risk reversal, proof requirements).
3. **Offer Backbone Architecture** — core systems, workflows, deliverables, required assets/approvals/technology/automations/documentation/SOPs/dashboards/communication systems; explicit **Immutable Components** (never change) vs. **Customization Components** (vary by industry/niche/size/goals/budget/maturity/growth stage).
4. **Client Journey Engineering** — full lifecycle, 17 stages: Discovery → Qualification → Audit → Strategy → Proposal → Agreement → Onboarding → Asset Collection → Implementation → Review → Optimization → Delivery → Reporting → Offboarding → Retention → Referral → Expansion. Each stage: goal, deliverables, timeline, dependencies, risks, communication/approval requirements, exit criteria.
5. **Internal Execution Journey** — agency-side fulfillment sequence: task-by-task owner/dependency/input/output/quality-check/approval mapping.
6. **Deliverable Engineering** — purpose, strategic value, format, owner, dependencies, required assets/approvals, revision policy, storage, delivery method, success metrics, quality standard, per deliverable.
7. **Timeline Architecture** — minimum/ideal/aggressive timelines, named delay-risk categories (approval, asset, technical, revision, client, resource).
8. **Communication Architecture** — channels, response SLAs, meeting/reporting cadence, approval windows, feedback/revision/escalation process.
9. **Client Constraint System** — named risk archetypes (Ghost Client, Unrealistic Client, Micromanager, Doubter, Know-It-All, Quitter, Result Ghoster), each with warning signals, operational risk, prevention/escalation/recovery systems.
10. **Quality Control System** — review stages, QA checklists, validation rules, approval gates, testing requirements, performance benchmarks.
11. **Revenue & Monetization Architecture** — maps Entry/Core/Premium/Enterprise offer tiers, retainer/productization/licensing/white-label/advisory/performance-fee/equity opportunities, upsell/cross-sell pathways, aligned to an "agency ascension path": Audit → Infrastructure → Optimization → Embedded Partnership → Enterprise Transformation.
12. **Scalability Engineering** — what must be standardized vs. customized vs. automated vs. delegated vs. productized vs. licensed vs. requires senior expertise, plus future AI opportunities.

Final output order: Offer Overview → Positioning Architecture → Backbone Architecture → Client Journey Mapping → Internal Execution Journey → Deliverables Mapping → Timeline Architecture → Communication Architecture → Client Constraint System → Quality Control System → Revenue & Monetization Architecture → Scalability Engineering → Automation Opportunities → Risk & Dependency Mapping → Offboarding & Retention System → Executive Summary.

## Offer #1: Revenue System Architecture (Sales Division)

**Offer Identity:** Predictable, repeatable, measurable sales infrastructure (CRM architecture, pipeline systems, outbound systems, deal stages, reporting dashboards, forecasting systems). Target client: $1M-$50M+ service businesses, B2B SaaS, agencies, professional services — note this is the chat's own stated target, written before the real Sector (01) ICP was confirmed; cross-reference against the real Tier 1 ($5M-$50M ARR) / Tier 2 ($1M-$10M ARR) B2B SaaS ICP once reconciling. Time-to-result: 14-45 days to operational system, 60-90 days to measurable revenue impact.

**Pricing (illustrative, owner's own strategic hypothesis, not validated against real deals):** Setup $7,500-$50,000. Retainer $3,000-$20,000/month.

**Positioning:** "We don't build sales funnels. We build revenue machines." Core promise: "In 30 days, you will have a sales system that produces predictable, measurable, repeatable revenue — without depending on any single sales rep." Risk reversal: 30-day system adoption guarantee (continue free until adopted, or final milestone waived).

**Backbone — 8 core systems:** CRM Infrastructure, Pipeline Management, Outbound Revenue System, Inbound Revenue System, Forecasting System, KPI & Reporting System, Accountability System, Sales Enablement System.

**Immutable components** (never change regardless of client): CRM field architecture standards, pipeline stage framework (minimum Lead→Opp→Proposal→Negotiation→Closed sequence), forecasting methodology (weighted pipeline × historical conversion × commit overlay), leading-indicator tracking, data hygiene rules, accountability cadence (weekly pipeline review, 1:1 manager framework, monthly forecast), SOP documentation standard, dashboard taxonomy.

**Customization components** (vary by client): CRM platform choice, pipeline stage count (4-12), stage qualification criteria (BANT/MEDDIC/CHAMP), outbound channel mix, deal-size tiers, sales cycle length, team size, tech stack integration, ICP/personas, compensation model, starting maturity level.

**Client journey:** all 17 stages fully specified (Discovery through Expansion) — see raw source for full per-stage goal/deliverable/timeline/risk/exit-criteria detail. Notably concrete: Audit (5-10 business days, 20-40 page report), Strategy (5-7 days, blueprint document), Implementation (14-30 days), explicit risk flag that **asset-collection delay is "the #1 timeline blocker in this offer."**

**Internal execution journey:** 8 fulfillment stages (Audit & Discovery → Strategy & Blueprint → Build: CRM/Infrastructure → Build: Dashboards/SOPs/Enablement → Client Validation & Revision → Training & Launch → Optimization & Handover → Post-Launch Support/Reporting, days 1-90), each broken into numbered steps with owner/dependency/input/output/quality-check/approval-gate detail — 7 named approval gates total across the full lifecycle.
