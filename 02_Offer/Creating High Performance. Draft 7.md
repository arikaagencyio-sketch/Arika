How does on create offers

You don’t “create” offers in the way most people think—you **engineer them as systems of value exchange**. If you approach this like packaging a product, you’ll stay average. High-performing offers are built like **conversion machines + delivery systems + behavioral triggers** working in sync.

Let’s break this at a high level, but with depth.

**1\. Start Where Most Get It Wrong: VALUE ASYMMETRY**

An offer exists when:

**Perceived Value >> Price + Effort + Risk**

So your job is not “what do I sell?”  
It’s:

  * What outcome is so desirable it distorts rational thinking? 
  * How can I reduce friction (time, effort, uncertainty)? 
  * How do I increase certainty of result? 



This leads to the core equation:

**Offer Strength = Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

If this isn’t engineered first, everything else collapses.

**2\. Define the CORE TRANSFORMATION (Not the Product)**

Weak builders describe:

  * “I sell marketing services” 



Elite builders define:

  * “I turn underperforming creators into revenue-generating brands in 90 days” 



You need:

  * **Before state (pain, frustration, identity)**
  * **After state (status, capability, measurable result)**



This becomes your **offer anchor**.

**3\. Identify the BUYING PSYCHOLOGY (Psychographic Layer)**

You already asked about psychographics—this is where it matters.

You need to map:

  * What do they _fear losing_? 
  * What do they _urgently want now_? 
  * What have they _already tried and failed at_? 
  * What do they _believe is the real problem_ (even if wrong)? 



This determines:

  * Messaging 
  * Pricing tolerance 
  * Urgency triggers 



Without this, even a good product won’t convert.

**4\. Construct the OFFER STACK (This is where money is made)**

You don’t sell “one thing.”  
You stack value components:

**Core Vehicle**

  * The main method (service, product, system) 



**Accelerators**

  * Tools, templates, frameworks 
  * Reduce effort 



**Guarantees**

  * Risk reversal (refunds, performance-based, conditional guarantees) 



**Bonuses**

  * Increase perceived value without major cost 



**Scarcity & Urgency**

  * Limited slots, deadlines, access conditions 



**5\. Engineer RISK REVERSAL (Most Underestimated Lever)**

People don’t buy because:

  * They doubt the result 
  * They fear wasting time/money 



So you remove that:

  * Outcome guarantee 
  * Conditional guarantee (“if you do X…”) 
  * Performance-based pricing 
  * “Done-with-you until result” model 



The stronger the guarantee → the higher the conversion rate.

**6\. Align DELIVERY WITH PROMISE (This is where most break)**

Most offers fail here:

  * Overpromise 
  * Underdeliver 
  * Kill retention and referrals 



You need:

  * A **clear mechanism of result**
  * A **repeatable delivery system**
  * A **timeline that is realistic but aggressive**



This is where your **sales system connects with onboarding + fulfillment**.

**7\. Price Based on VALUE, Not COST**

Wrong approach:

  * “How much does it cost me?” 



Correct approach:

  * “What is this outcome worth to them?” 



If your offer:

  * Generates $10K → pricing at $1K–$3K is rational 
  * Saves 100 hours → price against time value 



Pricing is a **signal** , not just a number.

**8\. Build the OFFER ECOSYSTEM (Advanced Layer)**

A single offer is weak.  
A system of offers creates scale.

You need:

  * Entry offer (low friction) 
  * Core offer (main revenue) 
  * Ascension offer (high ticket / premium) 
  * Retention layer (continuity, subscriptions) 



This becomes your **revenue architecture** , not just a one-off sale.

**9\. Test, Iterate, Optimize (Real Engineering Begins Here)**

No offer is perfect on first build.

You refine:

  * Messaging → does it resonate? 
  * Price → resistance points 
  * Structure → what increases conversion? 



Track:

  * Conversion rate 
  * Close rate 
  * Retention 
  * Refunds 



Offers evolve based on **market feedback, not assumptions**.

**10\. The Meta Layer: OFFERS AS STRATEGIC WEAPONS**

At the highest level, offers are not just for selling—they are for:

  * Market positioning 
  * Competitive domination 
  * Speed of revenue generation 
  * Customer acquisition cost reduction 



The best companies don’t just have better products—they have **better offers**.
