## Phase 3 — Backbone Architecture (synthesized)

**Core systems — 7 sub-audit systems, the most modular backbone of any offer:** Funnel Audit System, Sales Process Audit System, CRM/Data Audit System, Automation Audit System, Acquisition Channel Audit System, Team/Org Audit System, Offer/Pricing Audit System. Each is itself a discrete, potentially standalone diagnostic.

**Immutable components:** fixed fee and fixed timeline (7-14 days) regardless of findings complexity — the offer's value proposition depends on fast, predictable delivery, the fastest time-to-result of any offer by design; the written findings report is the only required deliverable (no implementation included); every finding must be tied to a quantified revenue/cost impact estimate, not a bare description of what's broken.

**Customization components:** which of the 7 sub-audits are in scope (full-stack vs. targeted single-area), depth of quantification (varies with client data access), report format (executive deck vs. detailed technical report).

## Phase 4 — Client Journey (synthesized, heavily compressed relative to every other offer)

Discovery → Scoping (which sub-audits) → Data Access/Collection → Analysis → Findings Report Delivery → Findings Review Call → Exit (explicitly named in Draft 28 as ascending to Infrastructure Implementation, the Stage 1 → Stage 2 path of the Ideal Ascension Model). Implementation/Optimization/Delivery/Retention stages don't apply the way they do elsewhere — this offer's "Delivery" *is* the findings report. The most structurally different offer from the standard 17-stage journey, even more so than offer #9 (Advisory).

## Phase 5 — Internal Execution Journey (synthesized — the fastest, most parallelizable sequence of any offer)

Data/Access Collection (Day 1-3) → Parallel Sub-Audit Analysis (Day 3-10, each sub-audit run largely independently, then synthesized) → Synthesis & Quantification (Day 10-12) → Report Production (Day 12-13) → Delivery & Review Call (Day 14).

**Team roster (standardized across offers #5-11 — see offer #5's Phase 5 note):** Client Partner, Strategy Lead, 2-3 Audit Analysts (parallelized per sub-audit), QA Specialist.

## Phase 9 — Client Constraint System (synthesized)

Highest-risk archetype: **Doubter** (commissions the audit but disputes the findings). This offer is structurally the **lowest-risk offer in the catalog** for most other archetypes — the short, fixed-scope engagement window limits exposure to Ghost Client, Quitter, and Micromanager risk. Worth flagging as a genuine finding: short, fixed-scope offers are inherently lower-constraint-risk than long infrastructure builds.

## Phase 10 — Quality Control System (synthesized)

Data-completeness gate before analysis begins; quantification-accuracy spot-check (each finding's dollar estimate reviewed by a second analyst); report-clarity gate (a non-technical executive must understand every finding without translation).
