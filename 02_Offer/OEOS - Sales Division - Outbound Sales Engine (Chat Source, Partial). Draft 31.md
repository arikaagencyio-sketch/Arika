## Offer #3: Outbound Sales Engine (Sales Division)

**Offer Identity:** Predictable outbound lead generation — cold email systems, LinkedIn systems, outbound scripting, infrastructure, appointment generation. "Pipeline on demand." Target client: B2B companies, $500K-$50M ARR, proven product-market fit, Seed-Series B primarily. Time-to-result: 7 days (infra live) → 14 days (first outreach) → 30 days (first appointments). Success criteria: 10+ qualified appointments/month by day 60, cost per meeting <$500, reply rate >3%.

**Pricing — real, 4-tier, ARR-segmented (most complete pricing structure of the 3 offers captured so far):**

| Tier | Setup | Retainer | ARR band | Commitment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $5,000 | $4,000/mo | Sub-$5M ARR | 3 months |
| Growth | $12,000 | $12,000/mo | $5M-$20M ARR | 6 months |
| Scale | $25,000 | $30,000/mo | $20M-$50M ARR | 12 months |
| Enterprise | Custom | $40,000-$100,000+/mo | $50M+ ARR | 12+ months |

Plus a performance-fee dimension named in Phase 1 (per appointment / per deal / revenue share) — not yet detailed before truncation. Each tier has a real "what's included" comparison (domains, inboxes, contacts sourced/month, sends/month, sequences, channels, appointment target, reply-management depth, dashboard tier, reporting cadence, SDR playbook depth, account manager dedication, risk reversal terms) — captured up to the Risk Reversal row before cutoff.

**Cross-offer finding — segmentation variable differs from offer #1.** Offer #1 (Revenue System Architecture) segments its 3 tiers by **rep count** (1-3 / 4-15 / 15-50+ reps). Offer #3 segments its 4 tiers by **ARR band** (sub-$5M / $5-20M / $20-50M / $50M+). Both are legitimate company-size proxies but use different variables — worth a deliberate decision on which the pricing-floor methodology (`02_Offer/OFFER_OS.md` §10) should standardize on, or whether different offer types legitimately warrant different segmentation variables (sales-infrastructure offers naturally correlate with rep count; outbound-volume offers naturally correlate with ARR/budget). **Not resolved — flagged for owner decision.**

**Cross-offer finding — phase-numbering drift.** This offer's own output labels its risk content "PHASE 6 — Risk & Failure Mode Analysis" and its pricing content "PHASE 7 — Pricing & Packaging Architecture" — different phase *numbers* than offer #1 used for analogous content (offer #1 used Phase 8 for risk registry, Phase 9 for pricing). The underlying OEOS prompt (`Draft 29`) defines Phase 6 = Deliverable Engineering and Phase 9 = Client Constraint System (named risk archetypes: Ghost/Unrealistic/Micromanager/Doubter/Know-It-All/Quitter/Result Ghoster clients) — neither offer #1 nor offer #3's "risk" sections actually match that Phase 9 framework; both instead produced a generic failure-mode/risk register under a different phase number. **This means the phase numbers in actual chat outputs don't reliably map 1:1 to the original prompt's phase numbers across different chat sessions** — content categories are recognizable and real, but don't assume "Phase N" means the same thing offer-to-offer. The named Client Constraint System archetypes (Ghost Client, Micromanager, etc.) from the original prompt have not actually appeared in any of the 3 offers captured so far — worth checking for in future offers or treating as a gap.

**Positioning:** "The operating system that turns cold outreach into a predictable, measurable growth channel." Core promise: "We will build, manage, and optimize a full-stack outbound system that delivers qualified appointments into your pipeline — predictably, every month." Risk reversal: 30-day infrastructure guarantee (work free until domains warm/inboxes sending/sequences live).

**Backbone — 8 core systems:** Infrastructure System (domains/DNS/inbox/warmup), List Sourcing System, Sequence Engineering System, Copy System, Delivery System, Inbox Management System, Appointment Booking System, Reporting System.

**Immutable components:** infrastructure-first approach (no domain ever goes straight to cold sending), multi-channel requirement (email + LinkedIn minimum), compliance framework (CAN-SPAM/GDPR/CCPA, no exceptions), 2-week minimum warmup protocol, reply detection/triage within 4 hours, automated bounce/unsubscribe handling, mandatory A/B testing (2+ variants per sequence), real-time dashboard/reporting for every client.

**Internal execution journey — most detailed of the 3 offers:** 32 numbered steps (vs. offer #1's phase-grouped ~50 steps and offer #2's phase-grouped ~60 steps) covering Discovery through Retention check-in, each with owner/dependencies/input/output/quality-check/approval-gate/**automation-opportunity** column (a column the other two offers' internal execution tables didn't explicitly include). Includes a Mermaid task-dependency flowchart, a full RACI team-ownership matrix (9 roles: Agency Lead, Strategy Lead, Infrastructure Specialist, Copywriter, List Specialist, Operations Specialist, Inbox Manager, Account Manager, plus Client Sales Lead/Leadership as Informed/Consulted), an 8-metric SLA framework with target/warning/critical thresholds and remediation per metric (e.g. deliverability rate target >97%, critical <90% triggers a send pause + full audit), and a 4-level escalation path (Level 1: standard issue, <2hr — up to Level 4: legal/compliance breach, immediate response).

**Risk register — 10 failure modes**, each with likelihood/impact/detection-method/prevention/mitigation/contingency/owner: domains blacklisted before warmup completes, infrastructure setup delay, low reply rate, low meeting show rate, client sales team doesn't follow up, list fatigue, client goes silent, missed payment, client ICP changes without notice, data breach, sending-platform outage, competitor copies approach. Also includes a **Risk Mitigation Budget Allocation** — % of retainer earmarked for infrastructure redundancy (10%), compliance/security (5%), continuous testing (15%), training (5%), client relationship management (10%), innovation/R&D (10%), operational buffer (5%) — a genuinely new concept not present in offers #1 or #2, worth considering whether it should generalize across all offers.
