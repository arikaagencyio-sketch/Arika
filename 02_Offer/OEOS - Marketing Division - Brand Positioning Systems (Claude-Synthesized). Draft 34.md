## Phase 2 — Positioning Architecture (synthesized)

Market position: the brand-and-narrative engineering arm of the "Revenue Infrastructure Partner" stack — distinct from a conventional branding/design agency in that narrative and pricing power are framed as revenue infrastructure, not aesthetics. Core promise: "We don't make you look different. We make you the obvious premium choice in your category." Value driver: pricing-power increase is the offer's stated Revenue Impact — every deliverable should trace back to that, not to subjective design taste. Risk reversal (synthesized, calibrated to the audit-as-entry-offer pattern corroborated elsewhere — `OFFER_OS.md` §3): a condensed positioning audit as the first deliverable, so direction is validated before full build-out begins.

## Phase 3 — Backbone Architecture (synthesized)

**Core systems:** Brand Strategy System, Messaging Architecture System, Visual Identity System, Executive/Founder Positioning System, Narrative Engineering System.

**Immutable components:** positioning locked before any visual design begins; executive/founder voice integration required (not generic brand voice); competitive differentiation analysis required before messaging is finalized; single source-of-truth brand guideline document as the terminal deliverable.

**Customization components:** visual depth (refresh vs. full rebuild), narrative complexity (single offer vs. multi-product portfolio), executive-positioning depth (founder-led vs. distributed leadership team), industry-specific visual/compliance norms.

## Phase 4 — Client Journey (synthesized, condensed)

Standard 17-stage journey, Day 1-60 (matching the stated 14-60 day range). Audit stage is a narrative-gap diagnostic rather than a technical/data audit (distinct flavor vs. infrastructure offers); Implementation splits into Messaging Development → Visual Development, with a stakeholder validation step before final delivery given the inherently subjective nature of creative judgment.

## Phase 5 — Internal Execution Journey (synthesized)

Discovery & Narrative Audit → Positioning Strategy (messaging pillars, differentiation map) → Creative Development (visual identity, voice guide) → Stakeholder Validation (internal + light market testing) → Delivery & Handover (brand guideline doc + team training session).

**Team roster (standardized across offers #5-11 — see note below):** Client Partner, Strategy Lead, Implementation Lead, Brand Strategist, Creative/Visual Designer, Copywriter, QA Specialist, Account Manager.

**Note on team-roster standardization:** offers #1-4 each used a different, independently-invented role roster (`OFFER_OS.md` §3, tracker item 39). For offers #5-11, since all are synthesized by the same author in the same pass, one consistent base roster (Client Partner / Strategy Lead / Implementation Lead / [domain specialists] / QA Specialist / Account Manager) is used throughout, with only the domain-specialist titles varying by offer. ## Phase 9 — Client Constraint System (synthesized)

Highest-risk archetypes for this offer: **Micromanager** (wants to approve every word/pixel — creative work invites this more than infrastructure work) and **Know-It-All** (founder insists on personal taste over market/differentiation data). Prevention: narrow approval gates defined in SOW (strategy sign-off vs. creative execution latitude); lead creative recommendations with the differentiation analysis, not aesthetic opinion.

## Phase 10 — Quality Control System (synthesized)
