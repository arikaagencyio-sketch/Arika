**3\. CASH FLOW & DSCR EVALUATION**

**The Core Rule:**

**Profit does not repay debt.**

**Cash flow repays debt.**

This distinction is foundational.

A profitable company can still collapse if:

  * Cash collection is weak
  * Receivables are delayed
  * Debt payments exceed liquidity timing
  * Working capital expands too aggressively



**DSCR — The Institutional Survival Metric**

Healthy institutional targets:

**DSCR**| **Institutional Interpretation**  
---|---  
<1.0| Dangerous  
1.0–1.25| Weak  
1.25–1.5| Acceptable  
1.5–2.0| Strong  
2.0+| Elite stability  
  
**Why DSCR Matters So Much**

DSCR measures:

  * Repayment survivability
  * Cash flow durability
  * Lending safety
  * Financial resilience



Low DSCR means:

  * Thin liquidity margin
  * High stress sensitivity
  * Vulnerability to revenue shocks



Banks fear:

  * Cash interruptions
  * Margin compression
  * Working capital spikes
  * Economic downturns

  * Cash timing risks are ignored

  * Lower interest
  * Strict covenants
  * Highest repayment priority



Best for:

  * Stable cash-flow businesses



Risk:

  * Liquidity pressure
  * Covenant breaches



**Mezzanine Financing**

Hybrid structure:

  * Debt + equity characteristics



Benefits:

  * Growth flexibility
  * Higher leverage access



Risks:

  * Expensive capital
  * Warrants/dilution



**Venture / Growth Equity**

Benefits:

  * No repayment burden
  * High growth acceleration



Costs:

  * Dilution
  * Governance control
  * Board influence



**Founder Equity**

Most expensive long-term capital psychologically because:

  * Ownership dilution is permanent
  * Future value may massively exceed current funding value
