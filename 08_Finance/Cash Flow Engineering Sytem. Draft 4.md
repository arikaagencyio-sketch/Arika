**4\. CASH CONVERSION CYCLE ENGINEERING**

**Core Formula**

Where:

  * DIO = Days Inventory Outstanding
  * DSO = Days Sales Outstanding
  * DPO = Days Payables Outstanding



**Why CCC Determines Scalability**

The Cash Conversion Cycle determines:

  * How long cash is trapped
  * How much working capital growth requires
  * Whether scaling creates liquidity pressure



**Elite Doctrine**

**The faster cash returns, the stronger the business.**

**Example**

**Weak CCC**

Inventory held: 90 days  
Customer payment: 60 days  
Supplier payment: 30 days  
  
CCC = 90 + 60 − 30 = 120 days

Business funds operations for 120 days before recovering cash.

Growth becomes liquidity-destructive.

**Elite CCC**

Inventory held: 15 days  
Customer payment: 7 days  
Supplier payment: 60 days  
  
CCC = 15 + 7 − 60 = -38 days

Business receives customer cash before paying suppliers.

This creates:

  * Self-financing growth
  * Reduced capital needs
  * Superior scalability



**CCC Optimization Levers**

**Reduce DIO**

  * Just-in-time inventory
  * Demand forecasting
  * SKU rationalization
  * Faster logistics
  * Vendor-managed inventory



**Reduce DSO**

  * Upfront deposits
  * Automated invoicing
  * Subscription billing
  * Auto-debit systems
  * Strict collections



**Extend DPO Responsibly**

  * Vendor negotiations
  * Procurement leverage
  * Payment scheduling
  * Supplier financing



**Hidden Working Capital Trap**
