The seven design laws

Hardened from EE's Visual, Motion & Interaction Language Systems into rules the audit can actually check. Every one is obeyed on this page.

Law 01

Asymmetric grid

12 columns; desktop leans 7/5, never 6/6; mobile stacks to full width; full-height sections use min-h-[100dvh].

Law 02

One type ratio

A single modular scale; every size derived from it; display headings scale fluidly with clamp().

Law 03

Restraint reads as luxury

One canvas, ≤2 accents, opacity tiers as the grey scale. Never pure #FFF on pure #000.

Law 04

One motion curve

A single easing language everywhere. Arika's settles rather than bounces.

Law 05

Respect reduced-motion

All scroll animation gated behind gsap.matchMedia(). Elegant, never nauseating.

Law 06

Real text in HTML

Headings are real text, not baked into effects — for findability and accessibility.

Law 07

Data-driven from day one

Content lives in content.config.ts, mapped over — never hardcoded in JSX.

Checked by /design-audit before any build ships.

Feasibility, told honestly

Every technique gets a tier

Naming the tier before you start is what stops you chasing an effect that needs a specialist — or paying for one you didn't need.

Tier 1

The build writes it directly

~80% of the premium feel. Lenis smooth scroll, SplitText reveals, pin-and-scrub, horizontal galleries, video-mask expansion. The Technical Director ships it.

Tier 2

Visual 3D, no hand-code

Real depth built in Spline and embedded — a cursor-reactive hero object, a scroll-driven camera push. Design (19) authors, Technical Director embeds.

Tier 3

Needs a creative developer

Bespoke shaders, navigable 3D worlds. The 3D Director flags it — then it's justified or cut. Never promised as shippable alone.

The fourth pillar · new

Beautiful, but findable

The one thing the department had no home for. Cinematic and discoverable aren't opposites — but you have to build for both deliberately.

SEO

Fast, crawlable, server-rendered, authoritative pages. The pool the AI reads from.

GEO

Authority, fact-consistency across every profile, original stats. The source the model trusts.
