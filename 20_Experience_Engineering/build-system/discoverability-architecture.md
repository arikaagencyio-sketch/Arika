## Part 1 — Frontend vs Backend (what you're actually assembling)

**Frontend** — everything the visitor sees and touches: layout, type, motion, 3D. Runs in their browser. This is where all the cinematic work lives.

**Backend** — the invisible engine: where data lives (products, orders, form submissions, accounts), the business logic, payments, security. Runs on a server.

**The non-coder rule: buy the backend, don't build it.** You assemble services, you don't engineer them.

| You need… | Don't build — use |
|---|---|
| A store (cart, inventory, checkout, payments) | **Shopify** |
| A contact form that actually delivers | A form service (e.g. **Formspree**) |
| Content you can update without rebuilding | A headless CMS (e.g. **Sanity**, or the CMS inside Framer/Webflow) |
| Accounts / login / a database | A code-generator lane (**Lovable**) on top of **Supabase** |

> **Arika note:** the agency already has real backends for its *operations* — Zoho Books (Finance), ClickUp (CRM), Notion (content briefs). This pillar is about the *website's* own backend needs (forms, CMS, any future Client Portal / Academy — the deferred Phase 2/3 of `ARIKA_WEBSITE_PROJECT.md`), which are separate.

### The bridge you must know: rendering
The very things that make a site cinematic — heavy JavaScript, content that appears only on scroll or inside animations — can make it **invisible to Google and AI**. Crawlers and AI don't read a page like a human; they scan for clean, extractable text.

**The fix:** server-side rendering (SSR), which **Next.js and Framer handle for you**, plus keeping real text in the HTML rather than baked into effects. Cinematic and findable aren't opposites — but you have to build for both deliberately. *(This is the same reason for EE design law #6, "real text in HTML.")*

## Part 2 — The discoverability stack

Three layers, stacked. Each sits on the one below.

| Layer | Goal | You're optimizing for | Core move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SEO** | Rank in Google's classic results | Google/Bing rankings | Fast, crawlable, useful, authoritative pages |
| **AEO** (Answer Engine Optimization) | Be *the answer* | Featured snippets, voice, AI answer boxes | **Answer the question first, then elaborate**; Q&A headings; FAQ schema |
| **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) | Be *cited inside* AI answers | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, AI Overviews | Authority, consistency, expert quotes, statistics, structured content |

**How they relate:** SEO gets you into the pool of pages the AI reads → AEO makes your content easy to extract → GEO makes the AI *choose you* when it writes the final answer. They overlap heavily; GEO is basically the generative-AI subset of AEO. Don't treat them as three separate expensive projects.

**Why it matters now:** over 60% of Google searches end without a click, a large and growing share of search runs through generative AI, and analysts expect a steep decline in classic search by 2028. Being the *source the AI trusts* is becoming as important as ranking.

**Two honest cautions:**
- **No one can guarantee AI placement.** Anyone who promises it is selling snake oil. Citations across AI platforms change month to month — visibility is real but unstable.
- **Volume doesn't work.** Publishing 100 keyword-stuffed posts does little for AI visibility. Ten well-structured, entity-rich, well-cited pieces beat 100 thin ones. *(This aligns with Content 04's own quality-over-volume pillar discipline.)*

## Part 3 — The checklist a non-coder can actually run

Nothing here needs code. Most can be done in your build tool or generated by AI.

**Findability (SEO + AEO + GEO share these):**
- Server-side rendered pages (use Next.js or Framer — don't ship a pure client-side app).
- A real `<title>` and meta description per page.
- One clear `<h1>`, logical `<h2>`/`<h3>` structure — not everything styled as a `<div>`.
- **Answer-first content:** lead with the direct answer, then expand.
- A genuine **FAQ section** using real questions your customers ask.
- **Schema markup** (Organization, FAQ, Product) — an AI can generate this JSON-LD for you to paste in.
- **Fact consistency (the consensus effect):** your name, address, phone, hours, and core offering must be *identical* across your site, Google Business, and every social profile. Conflicts make AI drop you. *(For Arika this means the site, Zoho Books org "Arika Agency," and every social account created under `GO_LIVE_CHECKLIST.md` item 23 must agree.)*
- **Authority signals:** a real author/founder bio with credentials; cite sources; include your own original stats or data where you can.
- A share image (Open Graph) so links look right when posted.
- Register with Google Search Console (free) to confirm you're indexed.

**Where AI actually pulls from — be present there:**
- LLMs lean heavily on **Reddit, YouTube, Wikipedia, and LinkedIn**. A presence on the relevant ones does more for GEO than another blog post. *(Cross-ref Marketing 03's Social Ecosystem, §10.)*

**Usability (accessibility — and it doubles as machine-readability):**
- Real semantic HTML (headings, buttons, alt text on images).
- Full keyboard navigability; visible focus states.
- Sufficient color contrast; respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (cinematic effects should soften or stop for users who disable motion — EE design law #5).
- Captions on video.
- *Bonus:* the same clean structure a screen reader needs is the structure crawlers and AI parse. **Doing accessibility right improves discoverability for free.** In many regions it's also a legal requirement — doubly so for a store.

## Part 4 — How this plugs into the pipeline

This layer isn't a separate stage — it's a set of constraints that ride along:

- **Sitemap stage (Spec System Station 2):** decide which pages carry answer-first content and FAQs.
- **Wireframe stage (Station 4):** make sure key text lives in the layout, not only inside animations.
- **Build stage:** choose an SSR-capable tool (Next.js/Framer); add schema; wire the real backend service.
- **Launch stage (Run-Sheet Phase 4):** Search Console, share image, fact-consistency pass across all profiles.
- **Audit gate (Station 6 / `design-audit`):** the QA & Performance Reviewer checks SSR, semantic headings, schema, and reduced-motion before ship.
- **Measure stage:** track not just Google rankings but whether ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini mention you when asked about your category.
