You don’t need to “deep dive everything” for every client—that’s how systems become bloated and slow. What you need is **selective depth based on where the client sits in the system and what outcome you’re engineering.**

Think of your folder as a **modular client operating system**. For each client, you zoom into the layers that actually move revenue or success for them.

Here’s how to decide _where to go deep_ :

**1\. Start With the Client’s Position in the Cycle**

From your earlier work on the client cycle, every client sits in one stage:

  * Acquisition (not yet a client) 
  * Activation (just closed) 
  * Delivery (active client) 
  * Expansion (upsell / retention) 
  * Advocacy (referrals, brand leverage) 



👉 Your deep dive depends on this.

**Example:**

  * New lead → focus on _offer + sales system_
  * Active client → focus on _delivery + success engineering_
  * Long-term client → focus on _expansion + retention systems_



**2\. The 5 Core Areas You Should Always Evaluate First**

Before going deep anywhere, scan these 5:

**1\. Offer Architecture (CRITICAL)**

  * What exactly are they buying? 
  * What outcome is promised? 
  * How is it packaged and priced? 



If this is weak → everything else breaks.

👉 Deep dive if:

  * Conversion is low 
  * Clients are confused 
  * Pricing resistance exists 



**2\. Client Segmentation**

  * Who exactly is this client? 
  * Which tier do they belong to? 



You already hinted at tiering clients—this is where it becomes real.

👉 Deep dive if:

  * You’re treating all clients the same 
  * Offers feel generic 
  * You can’t scale pricing 



**3\. Sales System**

  * How are they acquired? 
  * What channels convert them? 
  * What messaging works? 



👉 Deep dive if:

  * Leads exist but don’t convert 
  * Inconsistent revenue 
  * No predictable pipeline 



**4\. Customer Success / Delivery System**

  * How do they experience results? 
  * What is the journey after purchase? 



👉 Deep dive if:

  * Clients churn 
  * Poor results 
  * No repeat business 



**5\. Data & Feedback Loop**

  * What are you tracking? 
  * What improves the system? 



👉 Deep dive if:

  * You’re guessing instead of optimizing 
  * No clear metrics (CAC, LTV, conversion, retention) 



**3\. Then Go Deep Based on the Client’s Problem**

Now you choose **1–2 areas to go VERY deep** , not everything.

**If the problem is “we can’t close”**

Deep dive into:

  * Offer psychology 
  * Sales scripts 
  * Funnel structure 
  * Objection handling 



**If the problem is “clients don’t get results”**

Deep dive into:

  * Onboarding system 
  * Delivery workflows 
  * Success milestones 
  * Client communication loops 



**If the problem is “we can’t scale”**

Deep dive into:

  * Client segmentation & tiering 
  * Offer standardization 
  * Automation systems 
  * Operational calendar 



**If the problem is “low revenue per client”**

Deep dive into:

  * Pricing strategy 
  * Upsell / cross-sell systems 
  * Retention loops 
  * Value stacking 



**4\. The Depth Framework (How Deep You Go)**

When you choose an area, don’t stay surface level. Break it into:

**Layer 1: Strategy**

  * What is the goal? 



**Layer 2: Structure**

  * How is it designed? 



**Layer 3: Execution**

  * What actually happens daily? 



**Layer 4: Metrics**

  * How do we measure success? 



**Layer 5: Optimization**

  * How do we improve it? 



**5\. Your System Rule (Very Important)**

👉 Every client should NOT trigger a full-system deep dive.

Instead:

  * 100% system awareness 
  * 20–30% deep focus 
  * 1–2 priority bottlenecks solved 



That’s how you scale this into a real operating system.

Here’s a **5–10 minute Client Diagnostic Framework** you can run on any client.

**⚙️ THE 5–10 MINUTE CLIENT DIAGNOSTIC**

**Step 1: Define the Core Outcome (1 minute)**

Ask:

  * What result is this client paying for? 
  * What metric defines success? 



👉 Force it into one line:

“This client pays us to achieve: ______ by ______.”

**If this is unclear → STOP**  
That’s your first deep dive (Offer Architecture problem).

**Step 2: Identify the Current Constraint (2 minutes)**

Ask:

  * Where is revenue or results getting blocked? 



Choose ONE:

  * ❌ No leads → Acquisition problem 
  * ❌ Leads but no conversion → Sales problem 
  * ❌ Sales but poor results → Delivery problem 
  * ❌ Results but no retention → Expansion problem 
  * ❌ Happy clients but no referrals → Advocacy problem 



👉 Then confirm with:

“If we fix this, does everything else improve?”

If YES → that’s your focus.

**Step 3: Run the 5-Area Rapid Scan (3 minutes)**

Score each from **1–5 quickly (no overthinking):**

**1\. Offer Clarity**

  * Is the outcome clear, specific, and desirable? 



**2\. Targeting / Segmentation**

  * Is this client well-defined and correctly positioned? 



**3\. Sales System**

  * Is there a predictable way to acquire and close? 



**4\. Delivery / Success**

  * Can they consistently get results? 



**5\. Retention / Expansion**

  * Do clients stay, grow, or refer? 



👉 Now do this:

  * Lowest score = PRIMARY bottleneck 
  * Second lowest = SECONDARY bottleneck 



Ignore everything else.

**Step 4: Pinpoint the Exact Leak (2 minutes)**

Now zoom into the weakest area only.

Ask 3 sharp questions depending on the area:

**If OFFER is weak:**

  * Is the outcome vague? 
  * Is the value obvious vs alternatives? 
  * Is pricing aligned with value? 



**If SALES is weak:**

  * Are leads qualified? 
  * Is messaging aligned with pain/desire? 
  * Are objections handled? 



**If DELIVERY is weak:**

  * Is onboarding clear? 
  * Are there defined milestones? 
  * Is there accountability? 



**If RETENTION is weak:**

  * Are clients seeing continuous value? 
  * Is there a next step after delivery? 
  * Are you actively managing the relationship? 



👉 Identify ONE root issue only.

**Step 5: Assign a Fix (1–2 minutes)**

Turn the problem into a direct action:

“We will fix ______ by doing ______.”

Examples:

  * “We will fix low conversion by rewriting the offer promise and sales script.” 
  * “We will fix churn by redesigning onboarding and weekly check-ins.” 
  * “We will fix low LTV by introducing a tiered upsell system.” 



**🧠 OUTPUT (What you should have at the end)**

In under 10 minutes, you should have:

  * ✅ Core outcome defined 
  * ✅ One primary bottleneck 
  * ✅ One root cause 
  * ✅ One clear action
