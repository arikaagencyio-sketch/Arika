**Attention Engineering**

The system of designing stimuli that capture, direct, and sustain human attention in high-noise environments.

Modern marketing is no longer competing primarily on information.  
It competes on:

  * Cognitive bandwidth
  * Feed priority
  * Emotional interruption
  * Memory encoding
  * Retention duration



Attention is now the gateway layer before persuasion can even begin.

**Core Principle**

Attention is created when the brain detects:

  * Novelty
  * Relevance
  * Incomplete information
  * Emotional change
  * Threat/opportunity
  * Identity relevance
  * Pattern disruption



The brain constantly filters stimuli.  
Attention Engineering designs around bypassing or exploiting those filters.

**The Attention Stack**

**1\. Scroll Interruption**

Stopping passive motion.

Goal:  
Create a micro-pause.

Methods:

  * Unexpected visuals
  * Contrarian statements
  * Motion spikes
  * Scale distortion
  * Emotional facial expressions
  * Sudden contrast
  * Fast cuts
  * Silence breaks
  * Typography shock
  * Unusual framing



Examples:

  * “Nobody talks about this.”
  * Starting mid-conflict
  * Showing outcome before explanation
  * Extreme close-up visuals
  * Abrupt environmental change



This is the “thumb-stop layer.”

**2\. Curiosity Architecture**

Once attention is captured, curiosity sustains it.

The brain seeks closure.

This creates the:  
“open loop effect.”

Methods:

  * Incomplete information
  * Delayed reveal
  * Mystery framing
  * Unexpected contradiction
  * Tension without resolution
  * Sequential storytelling



Examples:

  * “This changed our conversion rate overnight.”
  * “We discovered something weird.”
  * “There’s one reason most brands fail here.”
  * “Wait until you see the ending.”



**3\. Narrative Tension**

Humans maintain attention through unresolved movement.

Attention collapses when outcomes become predictable.

Tension sources:

  * Conflict
  * Stakes
  * Transformation
  * Uncertainty
  * Time pressure
  * Social risk
  * Identity risk



Strong marketing constantly creates:  
“What happens next?”

**4\. Cognitive Reward Loops**

Attention sustains when viewers feel rewarded.

Micro-rewards include:

  * New insight
  * Humor
  * Surprise
  * Validation
  * Status gain
  * Emotional resonance
  * Fast learning
  * Dopamine spikes



This is why:

  * Short-form content loops
  * Rapid editing
  * Meme pacing
  * Insight stacking



perform well.

**5\. Identity-Relevant Attention**

The strongest attention is self-referential.

People notice things connected to:

  * Their ambitions
  * Their fears
  * Their tribe
  * Their status
  * Their worldview
  * Their frustrations



Examples:

  * “Designers are making this mistake.”
  * “Most founders ignore this.”
  * “If you grew up in the 2000s…”
  * “High performers know…”



Identity targeting dramatically increases retention.
