**1\. The Core Principle: One Continuous Revenue System**

  * Marketing → generates leads 
  * Sales → closes deals

That model is broken.

**Demand Creation → Demand Capture → Value Realization → Expansion**

**2\. The Aligned Customer Journey Architecture**

**Stage 1: Demand Creation (Marketing Owns)**

Goal: Create _qualified intent_ , not just awareness.

**What Marketing Must Do**

  * Define ICP + buying triggers (not demographics—situational intent) 
  * Pre-frame value before customer enters funnel 
  * Build problem-aware → solution-aware progression 



**Critical Alignment Point**

  * Messaging must match what Customer Success can actually deliver  
→ No overpromising (this is where most systems break) 



**Stage 2: Demand Capture (Marketing + Sales Hybrid)**

Goal: Convert intent into pipeline

**System Required**

  * Intent-based lead scoring (behavior > form fills) 
  * Segmented entry points (different journeys per persona/use case) 



**Alignment Mechanism**

  * Marketing qualifies _why they are buying_
  * Sales validates _if they can buy_



If those two don’t match → pipeline inflation + churn later

**Stage 3: Conversion (Sales Owns, Marketing Supports)**

Goal: Close deals that are **likely to succeed post-sale**

**Critical Shift**  
Sales should NOT optimize for:

  * Closing rate 
  * Revenue only 



They must optimize for:

  * **Customer success probability**



**Alignment Inputs from Marketing**

  * Content consumed 
  * Pain points identified 
  * Use-case intent 



**Output to Customer Success**

  * Structured handoff: 
    * Expected outcomes 
    * Use case 
    * Risk signals 



**Stage 4: Onboarding & Value Realization (Customer Success Owns)**

Goal: Deliver the promise made in marketing

**Where alignment usually fails:**

  * Customer Success gets a “closed deal,” not a “defined expectation” 



**System Required**

  * Time-to-Value (TTV) tracking 
  * First success milestone (not onboarding completion) 



**Feedback Loop to Marketing**

  * What value actually resonates? 
  * Where expectations mismatch? 



This should reshape messaging continuously.

**Stage 5: Retention & Expansion (Customer Success + Marketing)**

Goal: Turn customers into revenue multipliers

**Marketing’s Role Here (often ignored):**

  * Lifecycle marketing (email, content, community) 
  * Usage-triggered campaigns 
  * Expansion narratives 



**Customer Success Role:**

  * Identify expansion signals 
  * Trigger marketing plays
