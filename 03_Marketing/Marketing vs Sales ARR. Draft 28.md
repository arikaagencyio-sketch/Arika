Let’s get precise.

**1) First Principle: Marketing ≠ Sales Revenue Metrics**

Marketing’s financial system is **probabilistic and forward-looking** , while sales is **deterministic and closed revenue**.

  * **Sales ARR** = signed contracts → real revenue 
  * **Marketing ARR** = _influenced pipeline value_ → weighted future revenue 



So yes—**ARR in marketing is structurally different**.

Marketing should never claim full ARR unless it owns the entire funnel. Instead, it operates on:

  * Pipeline value 
  * Contribution % 
  * Velocity 
  * Efficiency ratios 



**2) The Core Financial Metric Stack for Marketing**

You don’t start with ARR—you _build up to it_.

**Layer 1: Unit Economics (Foundation)**

These define if marketing is even viable.

  * **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
  * **LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)**
  * **LTV:CAC ratio (target: 3:1+)**
  * Payback period (months to recover CAC) 



If this layer is broken → scaling is dangerous.

**Layer 2: Funnel Economics (Conversion Math)**

Here you quantify movement:

  * Cost per: 
    * Impression 
    * Click 
    * Lead (CPL) 
    * Qualified Lead (MQL/SQL) 
  * Conversion rates: 
    * Lead → MQL 
    * MQL → SQL 
    * SQL → Closed Won 



This is where marketing starts becoming financial, not creative.

**Layer 3: Pipeline Metrics (Marketing’s “ARR Equivalent”)**

This is the closest thing marketing has to ARR:

  * **Marketing Qualified Pipeline (MQP)**
  * **Pipeline Value ($)**
  * **Pipeline Coverage Ratio** (Pipeline ÷ Target Revenue, usually 3–5x) 



Then apply probability:

  * Weighted pipeline (based on stage conversion rates) 



This is **Marketing ARR (Modeled)**.

**Layer 4: Revenue Attribution**

Now you connect marketing to real money:

  * Sourced revenue (marketing originated) 
  * Influenced revenue (marketing touched) 
  * Assisted revenue (multi-touch attribution) 



This is where most companies get it wrong—over-attributing.

**Layer 5: Efficiency Metrics (Scale Layer)**

This determines if you can grow:

  * **CAC Payback Period**
  * **Marketing Efficiency Ratio (MER)** = Revenue ÷ Marketing Spend 
  * **ROMI (Return on Marketing Investment)**



At scale, this matters more than raw growth.

**3) Marketing ARR vs Sales ARR (Clear Distinction)**

**Sales ARR**

  * Closed deals 
  * Contractually binding 
  * 100% attributable 



**Marketing ARR**

  * Forecasted from pipeline 
  * Weighted probabilities 
  * Shared ownership 



**Formula (simplified):**

Marketing ARR =  
(Sourced Pipeline × Close Rate) + (Influenced Pipeline × Attribution %)

**4) The Cadence System (This is where most fail)**

Metrics without cadence = no control system.

You need **multi-layer cadence tied to financial signals** :

**Daily (Execution Signals)**

  * Spend vs budget

👉 Purpose: **Detect inefficiency early**

  * Funnel conversion rates

👉 Purpose: **Adjust tactics**

**Monthly (Financial Truth Layer)**

  * Pipeline value created 
  * CAC trends 
  * Marketing contribution to revenue 



👉 Purpose: **Validate ROI**

**Quarterly (Strategic Layer)**

  * LTV:CAC health 
  * Channel scaling decisions 
  * Budget reallocation 



👉 Purpose: **Scale or cut aggressively**
