Memetics Designing ideas to spread organically. A meme in marketing is: Compressible Repeatable Emotional Recognizable Adaptable

**Memetics**

Memetics is the engineering of self-propagating ideas.

A meme is not just a joke or internet image.  
In marketing, a meme is any unit of culture that spreads through imitation.

The goal of memetic marketing is:

  * Lower transmission friction 
  * Increase replication 
  * Encode identity 
  * Trigger participation 
  * Embed into culture 



**Core Properties of Memetic Ideas**

**1\. Compressibility**

The idea must be instantly understandable.

High memetic compression means:

  * Few words 
  * Clear symbolism 
  * Immediate recognition 
  * Fast cognitive processing 



Examples:

  * “Just Do It” 
  * “Think Different” 
  * “OK Boomer” 
  * The Nike swoosh 
  * The red Supreme box logo 



Compression creates:

  * Faster sharing 
  * Easier recall 
  * Higher survivability 



**2\. Repeatability**

People must be able to recreate it themselves.

Strong memes have:

  * Templates 
  * Formats 
  * Predictable structures 
  * Remix potential 



Examples:

  * TikTok sound formats 
  * POV videos 
  * “How it started vs how it’s going” 
  * Drake meme format 



A repeatable structure turns audiences into distributors.

**3\. Emotional Charge**

Emotion powers transmission.

Memes spread because they trigger:

  * Humor 
  * Status 
  * Anger 
  * Aspiration 
  * Belonging 
  * Shock 
  * Superiority 
  * Tribal identity 



Emotion = distribution fuel.

Without emotional energy, content stalls.

**4\. Recognizability**

Memes need distinctive visual or conceptual signatures.

This includes:

  * Symbols 
  * Phrases 
  * Colors 
  * Gestures 
  * Formats 
  * Audio cues 



Examples:

  * McDonald’s arches 
  * Apple minimalism 
  * Duolingo owl behavior 
  * “NPC” language online 



Recognition increases:

  * Familiarity 
  * Trust 
  * Share velocity 



**5\. Adaptability**

Memes survive through mutation.

Strong memetic systems allow:

  * Remixing 
  * Localization 
  * Community modification 
  * Platform adaptation 



A rigid message dies quickly.

A flexible meme evolves culturally.

**Replication Loop**

Exposure → Recognition → Emotional Trigger → Sharing → Remixing → Reinforcement

The best marketing systems shorten this loop aggressively.

Modern media environments reward:

  * Fast cognition

Algorithms amplify memetic behavior because:

Memetics is essentially:  
Behavioral virality engineering.

**Advanced Memetic Systems**

**Meme Stacking**

Combining multiple meme structures.

Example:

  * Humor + identity 
  * Status + controversy 
  * Aspiration + relatability 



This multiplies spread probability.

**Meme Hijacking**

Attaching your message to an existing cultural meme.

Example:  
Brands using trending TikTok audio formats.

Advantage:  
Borrowed attention.

Risk:  
Appearing inauthentic.

**Memetic Polarization**

Creating “for us / against them” dynamics.

This accelerates:

  * Tribal spread 
  * Community defense 
  * Organic debate 



Often used in:

  * Politics 
  * Streetwear 
  * Tech culture 
  * Creator brands

**Overcomplexity**

Too much explanation kills spread.

**Forced Virality**

**No Remixability**

If users cannot adapt it, the meme stagnates.
