What is stratergy

A **strategy** —especially in marketing—is not just a plan or a list of actions. It’s a **coherent set of choices that determines how you win** in a specific market.

At a high level:

**Strategy = where to play + how to win + what to prioritize + what to ignore**

**Core components of a real strategy**

**1\. Objective (What outcome matters)**  
Clear, measurable goal tied to business growth  
Example: Increase qualified leads by 40% in 6 months

**2\. Target Market (Where to play)**  
A sharply defined audience—not “everyone”  
You narrow down by:

  * Demographics 
  * Psychographics 
  * Behavior 
  * Buying triggers 



**3\. Positioning (Why you win)**  
Your unfair advantage in the customer’s mind  
This is not your product—it’s the perception you own

Example angles:

  * Fastest 
  * Cheapest 
  * Most premium 
  * Most specialized 



**4\. Value Proposition (What you deliver)**  
Clear reason someone chooses you over alternatives  
Usually framed as:

“We help [audience] achieve [result] without [pain]”

**5\. Competitive Strategy (How you beat alternatives)**  
You choose one dominant edge:

  * Cost leadership 
  * Differentiation 
  * Niche focus 



Trying all = weak strategy

**6\. Resource Allocation (What you double down on)**  
Where time, money, and attention go  
Real strategy says **no** more than it says yes

**What strategy is NOT**

  * Not tactics (ads, social media, SEO) 
  * Not goals alone 
  * Not a to-do list 
  * Not “posting consistently” 



Those are **execution layers** , not strategy

**Simple marketing example**

**Weak (no strategy):**  
“Let’s run Instagram ads and post content daily”

**Strong strategy:**

  * Target: First-time founders in tech 
  * Position: “Fastest way to validate your idea without wasting money” 
  * Channel focus: LinkedIn + founder communities 
  * Offer: Rapid MVP validation sprint 
  * Advantage: Speed + proven frameworks 



Everything else gets ignored

**The key principle**

**Strategy is about sacrifice.**  
If you’re trying to reach everyone, you don’t have one.
