**Framework 2: Attribution Logic**

Marketing's biggest challenge is proving **which channel drove revenue**. Three main models:

**Attribution Model**| **Logic**| **Best For**| **Weakness**  
---|---|---|---  
**First-Touch**|  100% credit to first channel that introduced the lead| Understanding which channels create awareness| Ignores nurture effort

**Recommended for B2B (like your sales system):**

**SECTION 21: Final Words — The Marketing Mindset**

**The 7 Principles of Great Marketing**

  1. **Revenue is the only scoreboard.** Brand awareness is important only if it leads to revenue. Every campaign, every channel, every dollar must tie back to pipeline or revenue.
  2. **Obsess over your customer.** Understand their pain, their language, their buying journey. Marketing is not about your product — it's about solving your customer's problem.
  3. **Test everything.** Assume nothing works until data proves it does. Run experiments, measure results, and let evidence guide decisions.
  4. **Simplify ruthlessly.** Clear messaging beats clever messaging. Simple processes beat complex ones. Fewer channels done well beat many channels done poorly.
  5. **Align with sales or die.** The handoff is where marketing success lives or dies. Build the SLA, share the goals, review the data together.
  6. **Content is the engine.** Every touchpoint is content. Every piece of content is an investment that compounds over time. Create once, distribute everywhere, measure everything.
  7. **Never stop learning.** Marketing changes constantly. What worked last year may not work this year. Stay curious, keep testing, keep adapting.

**SECTION 20: Final Words — The Marketing Mindset**

**The Principles That Never Change**

  1. **Marketing is a revenue function, not a cost center.** Every dollar spent must be traceable to pipeline or revenue. If you can't connect spend to outcome, stop spending.
  2. **The customer is the hero — you are the guide.** Great marketing doesn't say "Look how amazing we are." It says "You have a problem. Here's how to solve it. We can help."
  3. **Data over opinions.** Test everything. Trust the numbers, not the loudest voice in the room. If the data contradicts your instinct, trust the data.
  4. **Consistency beats intensity.** A blog post every week for 12 months beats 12 blog posts published in one week. Show up consistently, compound the results.
  5. **Marketing is never done.** Channels evolve. Competitors emerge. Buyers change. The moment you think you've "figured it out" is the moment you start falling behind.
  6. **Your brand is what people say about you when you're not in the room.** Invest in customer experience. One raving fan is worth a thousand ad impressions.
  7. **Simplicity scales.** Clear messaging, simple funnels, focused channel strategy — these compound. Complexity is the enemy of execution.
