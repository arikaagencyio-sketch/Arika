## 1. Mission
Build a marketing operating system that behaves like a coordinated intelligence network, not a disconnected task list.
The system must continuously run:
Intelligence -> Strategy -> Production -> Distribution -> Conversion -> Retention -> Measurement -> Optimization -> Scale.

## 2. Non-Negotiable Principles
1. Intelligence before execution: no campaign launches without validated signal.
2. Narrative coherence: all assets must align to one active positioning doctrine.
3. Conversion accountability: every workflow must map to a revenue outcome.
4. Closed-loop learning: experiments must produce decisions, not reports.
5. Governance over activity: escalation rules override speed when risk is high.
6. Channel as signal environment: channels are used as intelligence feeds, not just distribution.
7. Asset compounding: reusable systems and assets are prioritized over one-off output.

## 3. Runtime Model (Dual-Agent Architecture)
### Strategic Cognition Layer (Claude-class)
Owns analysis, positioning, segmentation, messaging doctrine, campaign logic, and optimization decisions.

### Execution Engineering Layer (Codex-class)
Owns implementation: funnels, automations, tracking, dashboards, content production pipelines, landing pages, and workflow orchestration.

### Interaction Contract
1. Strategy emits structured briefs (hypothesis, target, KPI, channel fit, constraints).
2. Execution translates briefs into deployable systems and assets.
3. Measurement emits truth data with decision recommendations.
4. Governance approves scale, rollback, or escalation.

## 9. Escalation Matrix
### Severity 1 (Critical)
Revenue system interruption, broken tracking, conversion collapse, compliance threat.
Action: immediate freeze, incident command bridge, rollback and root-cause report within 24 hours.

### Severity 2 (High)
Message-market mismatch, channel efficiency degradation, handoff quality drop.
Action: tactical hold on scale decisions, rapid diagnosis sprint, corrective plan within 72 hours.

### Severity 3 (Moderate)
Creative fatigue, reduced engagement, lagging experiment throughput.
Action: backlog reprioritization, test refresh cycle, monitoring and review.
