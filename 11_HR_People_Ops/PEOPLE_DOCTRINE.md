## 2. The doctrine, in five rules

**1. People are a consequence of revenue, not a route to it.**
`AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md`, owner-authored: *"We are not doing generic work. We are actually
doing the actual work. And we are not going to have things that are not being paid."* **A hire
that is not paid for by revenue that already exists is a bet, not a hire.** With zero clients,
the agency cannot currently afford anyone — and saying so is the doctrine working, not failing.

**2. Automate before you hire; hire what cannot be automated.**
The agency's own thesis. `arika-runtime` exists to make one person's reach larger. **The first
hire should be for something an agent genuinely cannot do** — and this OS has already found
those places precisely (§3).

**3. AI does not remove the human from a human gate.**
Class 3+ work requires human sign-off *"with no exceptions carved out by convenience or
urgency"* (Constitution §5). Every such gate is currently one person. **More agents make this
worse, not better** — each new agent adds gates to the same calendar.

**4. Structure follows the law, not the label.**
Calling someone a contractor does not make them one (`HR_RESEARCH.md` §2). **The structure must
survive the Control and Integration tests**, or the saving is a liability with a delay on it.

**5. The owner is a resource, and an unpaid, uncapped one is a single point of failure.**
Self-pay is a **capacity** question, not a nicety — see §4.

## 4. The owner as a resource — and self-pay

The owner named this explicitly: *"self-pay, because it's actually part of it."* It is, and here
is why it belongs in HR rather than only in Finance.

**The boundary (owner-confirmed 2026-07-15):**
- **HR (11) owns the doctrine** — *should* the owner be paid, what is defensible, and **what
  happens to capacity if they aren't**.
- **Finance (09) owns the money** — treasury, cash flow, and the actual payment, through
  `finance-treasury-agent` and `finance-cashflow-agent`.

### Why an unpaid owner is a capacity risk, not a virtue

`AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md` states it before this department existed: targets are *"treated as
non-negotiable, the same way **payroll**/bills/KPIs would be for any operating business."*

**The owner is the payroll.** An owner who takes nothing is not saving the business money — they
are **hiding the business's real cost of operation from itself**, and every margin, pricing
floor, and capacity number computed on that basis is wrong. `02_Offer`'s pricing-floor method
exists to find the *"do not quote below"* line. **A floor that assumes the founder works for
free is not a floor.**

And the failure mode is not financial. It is: **the single point of failure burns out, and there
is no second person.** Rule 5.

### What this department can and cannot say

**Can:** the owner should be paid; it should be a real, planned figure; every pricing and
capacity model should include it; and *"do-or-die"* is a statement of intensity, **not a
sustainability plan**.

**Cannot — and will not invent:**
- **A figure.** No amount, no band, no percentage. There is **zero revenue**, and a
  self-pay number invented against zero revenue is fiction.
- **The mechanism.** How a Kenyan owner should draw — salary vs. drawings vs. dividend — is an
  **accountant's question** and depends on the **unresolved entity structure**
  (`10_Legal/LEGAL_RESEARCH.md` §6, counsel Q7). → **accountant brief item C**
  (`HR_RESEARCH.md` §6).

**The honest position today:** the agency has zero revenue, so self-pay is **a doctrine waiting
for money**, and the first real revenue is when it becomes a live decision rather than a
principle. **`hr-owner-sustainability` (§5) exists to make sure that moment is noticed rather
than passed.**

## 6. Contract and freelance work

The owner's intent: *"we might onboard freelance photographers for marketing or anybody who can
be able to do a contract job for a short period."*

**The Cash Flow Calendar already anticipates this** — it tracks *"contractor costs"* as a real
category (`AGENCY_REVENUE_TARGETS.md`). This is consistent with the agency's design, not new.

**The rules:**
1. **Classification is decided by reality, not by the contract's title** (`HR_RESEARCH.md` §2).
   `hr-engagement-classifier` runs the Control / Integration / Substance tests **before**
   engagement, not after a dispute.
2. **🔴 The Integration Test is the live risk.** Arika **sells** creative production. A
   photographer producing assets inside a client deliverable is arguably **integral to the
   business**, not ancillary — the fact pattern most likely to be reclassified.
3. **Never choose contractor status because it is cheaper.** The ~7.5% employer add-on plus
   remittance machinery **is** the saving the KRA looks for as motive (`HR_RESEARCH.md` §3.3).
4. **No contractor agreement exists.** `10_Legal/templates/` has MSA/SOW/DPA/NDA — **no
   employment contract and no contractor agreement.** Counsel item, `HR_RESEARCH.md` §5.
5. **NDA first.** A freelancer sees campaign concepts and client material. `10_Legal/templates/
   NDA.md` **Part 2** exists for exactly this — *"contractors, freelancers, and partners"* is
   named as its real home.
6. **A contractor touching client personal data is a sub-processor** — `10_Legal/templates/
   DPA.md` Clause 5.4: the same obligations must flow down by contract, **and Arika remains
   fully liable**.

## 7. Remote work

Arika is **remote by default** and any future people will be. Two things follow, both real:

1. **Remote does not reduce employer obligations.** Written contracts, statutory deductions, and
   working-time rules apply regardless of where anyone sits (`HR_RESEARCH.md` §4).
2. **🔴 Outside Kenya is a different regime entirely.** Their country's employment law may apply;
   permanent-establishment and withholding questions arise; and whether they are a contractor is
   decided by **their** law, not Kenya's. **This is not remote — it is international
   employment**, and it is counsel question B.

**Why this is likely, not theoretical:** Arika's confirmed ICP is **B2B SaaS** with **US/EU**
clients, and the natural specialist hires — a developer, a designer — are exactly the roles most
often found internationally. **The doctrine's answer: do not engage anyone outside Kenya before
counsel has answered question B.**
